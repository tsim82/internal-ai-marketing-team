# Offer Formula Framework

The structured approach for building and pricing a high-converting offer. Used by the Offer Agent, Copywriter, and CFO Agent. Based on the principle that most offers fail not because of traffic or targeting but because the offer itself is not compelling enough.

---

## The One-Sentence Offer Test

Every offer must pass this test before anything else is built:

> "We help [specific audience] get [specific outcome] in [timeframe] without [main dread]."

If you cannot write this sentence with specific values in every bracket, the offer is not defined.

Bad: "We help business owners grow their business with our coaching program."
Good: "We help home service business owners book 10-15 qualified jobs per month in 60 days without cold calling or relying on referrals."

---

## The Four Offer Elements

**1. Dream Outcome**
What does the buyer want most? The more specific and emotionally resonant, the stronger the offer.
- Not: "More clients"
- Yes: "A full calendar of $2,000+ jobs every month without chasing leads"

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**
Why will this work for them when other things have not? This is the unique mechanism.
- What makes your approach different from what they have already tried?
- What removes the risk of failure?

**3. Time to Results**
How fast will they see results? The shorter the credible timeframe, the stronger.
- Make it specific and defensible. "Results in 2 weeks" needs proof.

**4. Effort and Sacrifice Required**
What do they NOT have to do? Removing objections from the offer itself ("without cold calling") is as important as the positive promise.

---

## Offer Equation (Hormozi Framework)

```
(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood)
÷ (Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice)
= Offer Value
```

To increase offer value:
- Increase the dream outcome (more specific, higher stakes)
- Increase perceived likelihood (guarantees, case studies, unique mechanism)
- Decrease time delay (faster result timeline, faster onboarding)
- Decrease effort and sacrifice (remove pain points from the process)

## The Value Stack

Every offer above $500 needs a value stack. The stack must total at least 3x the asking price.

Rules for value stacks:
- Every component must have a standalone price that is defensible and real
- The value assigned must be either market rate, outcome value, or competitor comparison
- Do not inflate values. Buyers notice. Overstated stacks reduce trust.
- Lead with the component that solves the most painful or important problem
- Bury the weakest component mid-stack

Example format:
```
What You Get:
- [Component 1] (Value: $X)
- [Component 2] (Value: $X)
- [Component 3] (Value: $X)
Total Value: $X
Your Investment: $X
```

## Guarantee Construction

A guarantee reduces perceived risk. The stronger and more specific the guarantee, the higher the conversion rate.

Types ranked by strength:
1. Outcome guarantee - "You'll get X, or we'll work with you until you do"
2. Money-back guarantee - "If you do not get X by Y date, full refund"
3. Service guarantee - "We'll do this specific thing as part of the promise"
4. Partial refund guarantee - "If not satisfied, we'll refund Z%"
5. Satisfaction guarantee - "If you're not happy, let us know" (weakest)

Requirements for any guarantee:
- Name the guarantee specifically
- State the exact condition (what triggers it)
- State the exact remedy (what happens)
- State any qualification conditions (what the buyer must do to be eligible)

Never put a guarantee in copy without the QA Agent approving the language and confirming it is enforceable.

---

## Urgency and Scarcity Rules

Urgency and scarcity increase conversions only when they are real and enforced.

Real urgency/scarcity:
- Capacity limit (you can only serve X clients at once - enforce it)
- Cohort or launch model (enrollment closes on a specific date - stick to it)
- Pricing deadline (price increases after a date - actually increase it)
- Bonus deadline (bonus expires at a specific time - remove it after)

Fake urgency is a legal and trust risk. A countdown timer that resets is a compliance violation and a QA failure.
