# Content Pillar Framework

The system for organizing all organic social content into categories that serve different purposes. Used by the Social Media Agent, Copywriter, and Analytics Agent. Content without a pillar system produces random content that does not build toward a goal.

---

## The Five Pillars

| Pillar | % of Calendar | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Education | 40% | Build trust and authority by teaching something genuinely useful |
| Social Proof | 20% | Show real results and real clients to build belief |
| Brand/Story | 20% | Build connection and personality - who you are, not just what you do |
| Engagement | 10% | Generate comments, saves, shares - drive algorithm signals |
| Offer/Promotion | 10% | Direct promotion of services, calls, or lead magnets |

Adjust percentages during launch periods. A campaign launch month might shift to 25% offer content for weeks 3-4.

---

## Pillar Definitions

**Education (40%)**
Content that teaches something the target audience would find useful. The reader leaves knowing something they did not know before.

Good education content:
- Teaches a real skill or process
- Gives a framework or mental model
- Explains a common mistake and the fix
- Answers a question the audience actually has

Bad education content:
- Generic tips anyone could write
- Content that could appear in any niche
- Lists for the sake of lists

**Social Proof (20%)**
Content that shows real results from real clients. The reader sees someone like them getting the outcome they want.

Good social proof content:
- A specific before and after with numbers
- A short client story with the shift that made the difference
- A testimonial or screenshot (with permission)
- A case study result

Bad social proof content:
- Vague claims like "My client did amazing!"
- Fabricated or exaggerated results
- Social proof without context (what were they struggling with? what changed?)

**Brand/Story (20%)**
Content that builds connection by showing the person or personality behind the business.

Good brand/story content:
- A specific story about a decision, lesson, or failure
- A take or opinion that feels genuine and not corporate
- Behind-the-scenes of how work is done
- A personal experience that connects to the audience's situation

Bad brand/story content:
- Vague motivational content
- Generic "my journey" posts without a specific point
- Personal content that has no connection to the audience's interests

**Engagement (10%)**
Content designed to generate comments, saves, shares, or other algorithmic signals.

Good engagement content:
- A question the audience has a strong opinion about
- A poll or this/that choice
- A controversial take that invites response
- A "save this" framework or resource

Bad engagement content:
- "Like and comment if you agree!"
- Engagement bait that does not connect to the brand
- Questions the audience does not actually care about

**Offer/Promotion (10%)**
Content that directly promotes a service, call, lead magnet, or other offer.

Good offer content:
- A direct pitch for the service with a clear CTA
- A limited-time or capacity-based promotion
- A lead magnet or freebie push
- An invitation to book a call

Bad offer content:
- Treating every post as a sales pitch (this should be 10%, not 50%)
- Promotional content disguised as educational content
- A hard sell without any context or social proof

## The Weekly Rhythm

A 5-posts-per-week schedule using the pillar split:

| Day | Pillar |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Education |
| Tuesday | Social proof |
| Wednesday | Education |
| Thursday | Brand/story |
| Friday | Engagement or offer |

This rhythm prevents back-to-back promotional posts and keeps the feed varied.

---

## Content Pillar by Platform

The pillar system applies across all platforms. The format and length change by platform - the pillar does not.

| Platform | Format Notes |
|---------|-------------|
| Instagram | 50-150 words, heavy white space, 3-5 hashtags |
| Facebook | 100-300 words, links in comments, engagement-focused CTA |
| LinkedIn | 150-400 words, one idea per line, heavy white space, 0-3 hashtags |
| TikTok / Reels | Caption supports the video - 50-100 words max |
