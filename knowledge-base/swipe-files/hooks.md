# Hook Swipe File

A reference library of hook structures for ads, social posts, video scripts, and email subject lines. These are frameworks and structures, not word-for-word copy to paste. Adapt each hook to the specific audience, offer, and platform.

---

## Hook Types and When to Use Them

| Hook Type | When to Use | Expected CTR Range |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Curiosity gap | Cold traffic, broad audiences | Moderate - high |
| Pain mirror | Problem-aware audiences, retargeting | High for the right audience |
| Proof/result | Warm audiences, anyone with objections about whether it works | High with credible proof |
| Contrarian | When the market is saturated with the same message | Variable - polarizing |
| Bold claim | When the claim is genuinely surprising and true | High if claim is credible |
| Direct benefit | Offer-aware audiences, retargeting, direct response | High for ready-to-buy audience |

---

## Curiosity Gap Hooks

Structure: [Partial information that makes the reader want the rest]

Examples:
- "The one thing I changed that took my CPL from $85 to $22"
- "Most service businesses never figure this out about paid ads"
- "I was wrong about [common belief] for 3 years. Here's what I finally learned."
- "Why your competitors are getting leads and you're not (it's not what you think)"
- "The [timeframe] fix that saved my client's entire ad account"
- "What nobody tells you about [common tactic] until it's too late"
- "The reason [desirable outcome] feels impossible (and the actual fix)"

---

## Pain Mirror Hooks

Structure: [Reflect the exact situation or feeling the reader has right now]

Examples:
- "Tired of paying for leads that never pick up the phone?"
- "If your ad spend goes up but your leads don't, here's why"
- "You're not getting leads because of your ads. You're getting them because of your follow-up."
- "Running ads but can't tell if they're working? This is why."
- "Your calendar should be full. Here's why it isn't."
- "[Specific audience], if your pipeline feels unpredictable, this is the likely reason."
- "You post consistently. Your engagement rate is fine. But no one's booking. Sound familiar?"

---

## Proof and Result Hooks

Structure: [Specific result, specific person or situation, or both]

Examples:
- "My client went from 3 booked calls per week to 14. Here's what changed."
- "We spent $1,200 in ads and closed $18,000 in new jobs. Here's the breakdown."
- "This [niche] business owner hasn't relied on referrals in 8 months."
- "47 leads in 30 days. $0 in organic content. Here's how."
- "From 0 to fully booked in 6 weeks. Not magic. Just this system."
- "She was about to quit her business. Then this happened."

---

## Contrarian Hooks

Structure: [Challenge the conventional wisdom the audience believes]

Examples:
- "Stop boosting posts. It's making your results worse."
- "More followers does not mean more clients. Here's what does."
- "The agency told him he needed a new website. He needed better follow-up."
- "Most people say you need to go viral to grow. You don't."
- "Running ads for 6 months without profitable results? It's probably not the ads."
- "Your close rate problem is not a sales problem. It's a lead quality problem."
- "The 'just test more creatives' advice is keeping your CPL high."

---

## Bold Claim Hooks

Structure: [Make a specific, verifiable, surprising claim]

Examples:
- "The first 5 minutes after a lead opts in determines whether you'll close them."
- "Most ad accounts waste 40-60% of their budget on underperforming ads that could be caught in the first 72 hours."
- "One funnel change increased our show rate from 55% to 78%."
- "The average business owner loses $3,000-$8,000 per month in revenue from slow lead response time."
- "8 out of 10 service businesses have the same speed-to-lead problem."

---

## Direct Benefit Hooks

Structure: [State exactly what the reader gets, immediately]

Examples:
- "Book 10-15 qualified calls per month with paid ads. Here's the system."
- "Get your first lead in under 48 hours. Here's exactly what to do."
- "Want a calendar that fills itself? This is how."
- "Cut your CPL in half without changing your budget. Here's the process."
- "Double your show rate this week. One change."

---

## Email Subject Line Hooks

Shorter format. Under 50 characters ideally.

Curiosity: "The mistake you're probably making right now"
Pain mirror: "Why your CPL keeps going up"
Proof: "47 leads. 30 days. Here's what we did"
Contrarian: "Stop A/B testing your ads"
Direct: "New: how to get your calendar full"
Question: "Is your landing page the problem?"
Bold: "You're losing 40% of your leads before they see an ad"

## Platform Notes

**Meta Ads (first line of primary text):**
This is the hook. Treat it like a headline. Must stop the scroll in 1-2 lines.

**Instagram post (first line before "...more"):**
This must work as a standalone sentence that creates a reason to tap "more."

**LinkedIn (first line):**
Must earn the "see more" click. Direct and specific outperforms vague and mysterious.

**Short-form video (first 1-3 seconds):**
Spoken hook + optional text overlay. Works fastest when it names the exact audience or exact problem in the first sentence.

---

## Usage Notes

- Adapt to the audience: replace [niche] and [audience descriptor] with the specific target
- Never use a hook that you cannot back up. A proof hook needs real proof.
- Test multiple hook types for the same offer. The winner is rarely what you expect.
