# Email Subject Line Swipe File

A reference library of high-performing email subject line structures. These are structures and patterns, not word-for-word copy. Adapt each one to the specific niche, audience, and context.

---

## Subject Line Rules (from brand/voice-rules.md)

- Under 50 characters for best preview display on mobile
- Preview text should add information the subject line does not repeat
- Subject lines that tease a specific thing outperform vague ones
- Do not use "RE:" or "FWD:" falsely - it destroys trust
- Avoid ALL CAPS except for one word for emphasis

---

## High-Performing Subject Line Structures

### Curiosity / Open Loop

- "The [niche] mistake I made that cost me [loss]"
- "Why I stopped [common thing] (and what I do instead)"
- "What I learned after talking to [number] [audience type]"
- "The question I get more than any other"
- "This surprised me"
- "Something I've been meaning to tell you"
- "I was wrong about [topic]"
- "Here's what nobody talks about when it comes to [topic]"
- "[Number] things I'd do differently if starting over"

### Pain Mirror

- "Still struggling with [specific problem]?"
- "Is this why your [metric] is [bad state]?"
- "What's really killing your [desired outcome]"
- "The [problem] you might not know you have"
- "If you're not getting [desired result], here's why"
- "Your [process] has a problem. Here's what it looks like."

### Proof and Results

- "[Specific result] in [timeframe] - here's how"
- "How [client/person] went from [before] to [after]"
- "[Number] [results] in [timeframe]: what worked"
- "Case study: [specific outcome] from [specific starting point]"
- "What [client] did differently (and why it worked)"

### Contrarian / Pattern Interrupt

- "Stop [common tactic]. It's not working."
- "The [popular belief] is wrong. Here's what's actually happening."
- "Everyone says [X]. I disagree. Here's why."
- "The advice that's keeping your [metric] stuck"
- "What most [niche] experts get wrong about [topic]"

### Direct Benefit

- "How to [desired outcome] without [dread]"
- "[Timeframe] to [result]: the exact steps"
- "Get [specific result] by [deadline or timeframe]"
- "[Number] ways to improve your [metric] this week"
- "The [descriptor] way to [outcome]"

### Question Format

- "Is your [process/system] the problem?"
- "What if [common struggle] was actually fixable?"
- "Are you making this [niche] mistake?"
- "Have you tried this yet?"
- "What would [outcome] mean for your business?"

### Scarcity / Deadline (real only)

- "Closing [number] spots [date]"
- "Last [timeframe] to get [thing]"
- "[Thing] goes away [date]"
- "Final reminder: [specific deadline thing]"

### Personal / Story

- "I almost gave up on [thing]. Then this happened."
- "The [timeframe] that changed how I think about [topic]"
- "Real talk: [honest observation about the reader's situation]"
- "A quick story about [relatable situation]"

## Preview Text Structures

Preview text appears after the subject line in most email clients. It should extend the subject, not repeat it.

If subject is: "Why I stopped [X]"
Preview could be: "And what I do now instead (it took me too long to figure this out)"

If subject is: "47 leads in 30 days"
Preview could be: "Here's the exact breakdown, including what almost didn't work"

If subject is: "Is your landing page the problem?"
Preview could be: "There's a 3-part test I run before touching the ads. Here's how to do it."

## What Makes a Subject Line Fail

- Too vague: "Check this out" - nothing to click on
- Too corporate: "Important update regarding your account"
- Too salesy: "LAST CHANCE! Get 50% off TODAY ONLY!!!"
- Too generic: "How to get more leads" - could be from any marketer in any niche
- Misleading: Promise something in the subject you do not deliver in the email
