# Ad Copy Structure Examples

Reference structures for Meta ad copy. These show the format and flow for different copy types. Not finished ads - structures the Copywriter adapts for each offer and audience.

---

## Structure 1: Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS) - Short Form

**Best for:** Cold traffic, pain-aware audiences, lead gen offers

```
[Hook - first line, must stop the scroll]
If you're [situation], you've probably [consequence].

[Agitate - make the pain more specific and real]
Most [audience type] spend months [failed approach] without [desired result].

[Solve - introduce the mechanism]
The problem isn't [common scapegoat]. It's [real cause].

[Result - what's possible]
When [audience type] fix this one thing, [specific result] tends to follow.

[CTA]
Click below to [specific action].
```

**Example filled in:**
```
Paying for leads that never pick up the phone?

If you're running ads for your contracting business, you've probably
had months where the CPL looks fine but the calendar stays empty.

Most contractors spend thousands testing new audiences and creative
without touching the follow-up. That's where 80% of the money leaks.

When contractors fix their speed-to-lead and follow-up sequence,
show rates jump from 50% to 75%+ in a few weeks.

Click below to see how the system works.
```

## Structure 2: Proof-First Story - Medium Form

**Best for:** Warm audiences, higher-ticket offers, anyone who needs proof before clicking

```
[Proof hook - specific result upfront]
My client [Name/descriptor] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe].

[Setup - who they were and what was broken]
When we first talked, [describe the before situation in specific terms].

[The shift - what changed]
The one thing that made the difference: [mechanism or key change].

[Result with detail]
[Specific numbers]. [Timeline]. [Downstream effect].

[Parallel/you hook]
If you're [similar situation], this is probably what's holding you back too.

[CTA]
[What to do next].
```

**Example filled in:**
```
My client went from 3 booked calls per week to 14 in 30 days.

When we first talked, she was spending $2,800/month on Meta ads
and getting leads - but barely 40% of them were showing up on calls.

The one thing that made the difference: a 6-touch follow-up sequence
that ran in the first 7 days after opt-in, with the first touch firing
in under 5 minutes.

14 booked calls per week. $31 CPL. 74% show rate. And she stopped
paying a virtual assistant to manually chase leads every day.

If your ads are generating leads but your calendar doesn't show it,
the problem is probably in the follow-up, not the ads.

Click below to see the system.
```

## Structure 3: Contrarian Hook - Medium Form

**Best for:** Saturated markets, skeptical audiences, people who have heard every ad before

```
[Contrarian hook - challenge what they believe]
[Common belief] is wrong.

[Establish the common approach and why it fails]
Most [audience] [common approach]. And it [consequence].

[The real reason it fails]
The reason isn't [assumed cause]. It's [actual cause].

[The better way - brief mechanism]
[What actually works and why].

[Proof or credibility]
[Result or social proof that backs this up].

[CTA]
[What to do next].
```

**Example filled in:**
```
More leads is not your problem.

Most service businesses when results stall run more ads,
test more audiences, and try new creative. And CPL keeps climbing.

The reason isn't the ads. It's that 60% of leads who don't book
never received a follow-up within the first hour.

Fix the follow-up before touching anything else. That's the lever
most agencies never talk to you about because they don't own the CRM.

We handled exactly this for a roofing company in Texas - same ad
spend, 2.4x the booked calls in 3 weeks.

Click below if you want to see how it works.
```

## Structure 4: VSL/Video Hook (spoken, first 10 seconds)

**Best for:** Video ads, Reels, TikTok, YouTube pre-roll

```
[0-3s spoken hook]
"[Audience-specific direct address or specific provocative statement]"

[3-7s setup]
"[One sentence that describes the exact situation they're in]"

[7-10s promise]
"[Specific result or what's coming in this video]"
```

**Example filled in:**
```
[0-3s] "If you're a home services contractor running ads, I want to show
you why your CPL keeps going up even when your ads are working."

[3-7s] "Most contractors I talk to have a lead problem on the surface
and a follow-up problem underneath it."

[7-10s] "In the next 3 minutes I'm going to show you the sequence
that cuts our clients' lead-to-booked gap in half."
```

## Headline Structures (for Facebook/Meta ads and landing pages)

These are the main headline above the body copy. Different from the first line of body copy.

Direct: "[Specific audience] Get [Specific Result] in [Timeframe]"
Question: "Are You a [Audience] Who [Has the Problem]?"
How-to: "How to [Achieve Result] Without [Main Dread]"
Number: "[X] [Audience] Booked [X] Calls in [Timeframe] Using This"
Proof: "From [Before] to [After] in [Timeframe] - Here's How"
Contrarian: "Stop [Common Tactic]. Do This Instead."

## CTA Examples

**For lead gen (download / opt-in):**
- "Click 'Learn More' to get the free guide."
- "Hit the button below to see how it works."
- "Download the guide - it takes 30 seconds."

**For call booking:**
- "Click below to book a free 20-minute call."
- "See if this is a fit. Book a call below."
- "Apply below - we take [X] clients per month."

**For VSL / webinar:**
- "Click below to watch the full breakdown."
- "Watch the 12-minute video below."
- "Click to see the full case study."

---

## Notes

- CTA language should match the next step exactly ("Book a call" if the destination is a calendar, not "Learn more")
- All specific numbers in copy must be real and approved by the claims-review process
- The hook is the most important line - test at least 3 hook types per offer
