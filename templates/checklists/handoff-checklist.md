## 1. Completeness

- [ ] The deliverable matches what was requested in the brief or task
- [ ] All sections of the brief are addressed - nothing is skipped
- [ ] All required variations are included (e.g., 3 headline variations, 3 subject line options)
- [ ] No [TBD], [INSERT], [PLACEHOLDER] text remains in client-facing content
- [ ] No placeholder variables remain unfilled (e.g., [CLIENT NAME], [PRICE])
- [ ] Supporting context the receiver needs is included or linked

## 2. Voice and Formatting (Written Deliverables)

- [ ] No em dashes anywhere in the copy
- [ ] No banned words (delve, revolutionize, supercharge, etc. - full list in voice-rules.md)
- [ ] No corporate or hype language
- [ ] Contractions used where natural
- [ ] Plain English - no jargon unless the audience uses that term themselves
- [ ] No rhythmic three-part phrasing
- [ ] Tone matches the audience and the context

## 3. Claims and Compliance (Client-Facing Copy)

- [ ] All specific results and numbers are real and documented
- [ ] Income or earnings claims have appropriate disclaimers
- [ ] Guarantees are enforceable and clearly stated
- [ ] No urgency or scarcity elements are fabricated
- [ ] If claims review was required, it was completed and all items are PASS

**Flags found:**

---

## 4. Deliverable-Specific Checks

**For ad copy:**
- [ ] Hook is in the first line of primary text
- [ ] CTA matches the destination (book a call → calendar link, not "learn more")
- [ ] Headline variations are genuinely different, not rephrasing the same idea
- [ ] Character limits are met for all fields

**For landing page copy:**
- [ ] Headline matches the ad that sends traffic to it
- [ ] Social proof is included
- [ ] CTA appears above the fold and at the bottom
- [ ] Mobile reading experience considered (short paragraphs, white space)

**For email sequences:**
- [ ] Each email has a single clear purpose
- [ ] Subject line variations provided (minimum 3)
- [ ] Preview text is included and does not repeat the subject line
- [ ] Unsubscribe / opt-out language is in the sequence (minimum once)
- [ ] Stop conditions are documented

**For creative briefs:**
- [ ] Platform and dimensions specified
- [ ] Concept type is clear and specific (not vague)
- [ ] Text overlay is 5 words or fewer
- [ ] B-roll shots are described specifically (not "person at computer")

**For n8n workflow plans:**
- [ ] All nodes are named
- [ ] Trigger, path, and output are clearly defined
- [ ] Error handler path is included
- [ ] Test payload is documented
