# YouTube Script

## Purpose

Write a complete, chapter-structured script for a YouTube long-form video (8-30 minutes). YouTube scripts differ from VSLs and short-form content - they must deliver genuine value while retaining viewers through multiple retention devices. The output is a full word-for-word script with chapters, retention hooks, and a thumbnail brief.

## Used By

- Video Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (writes the script)
- Creative Design Agent (writes thumbnail brief from the script)

## Trigger

Use when a YouTube video needs to be scripted from scratch, when a webinar or interview needs to be adapted into a YouTube-native format, or when a client is building a YouTube content strategy.

## Inputs Needed

- Video topic and main point - required
- Target audience (who is this for, what level of expertise?) - required
- Target length - required
- Key points or framework the video covers - required
- Any proof, case study, or visual data to reference - helpful
- Keyword or search query the video targets (from YouTube SEO) - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Plan the title and hook**

YouTube title formula: [Specific result] + [Timeframe or condition]
Examples:
- "How We Generated 47 Leads in 3 Days With No Ad Spend"
- "The Cold Email System That Books 5 Calls a Week (Step by Step)"
- "Why Your Facebook Ads Stopped Working (And How to Fix It)"

Avoid: "How to do marketing" or "Tips for business owners." Too vague.

**Step 2: Write the cold open**

The first 30 seconds must hook the viewer before any intro, channel branding, or "please like and subscribe."

The cold open:
- Makes a specific promise ("By the end of this, you'll know exactly why X is happening and what to do about it")
- Establishes why this viewer should care
- Creates a reason to stay ("And I'll show you the case study that changed how I think about this")

Do not start with: "Hey everyone, welcome back to my channel." Start with the result or the problem.

**Step 3: Brief credentials (30s - 2min)**

After the hook, spend 30-60 seconds establishing why the viewer should trust this video:
- Who you are and your relevant experience
- One specific result or example that proves expertise
- Brief preview of what the video covers

Keep this tight. Credentials that drag past 90 seconds lose viewers.

**Step 4: Structure the chapters**

Aim for 3-6 main chapters. Each chapter:
- Has a clear, named topic
- Delivers on a specific sub-promise
- Ends with a teaser for the next section ("And in section 4, I'll show you the one thing most people miss...")

**Step 5: Write retention hooks every 2-3 minutes**

Viewers drop off. Every 2-3 minutes, insert a retention device:
- Pattern interrupt: switch visual, introduce a new story, change the format
- Open loop: tease something coming later in the video
- Question: ask the viewer something that makes them want to see the answer
- Re-hook: "If you're still here, this next part is the most important thing I'm going to say"

**Step 6: Write the CTA**

End-of-video CTA (last 60-90 seconds):
1. Summarize what was covered
2. Tell them what to do next (subscribe, comment, watch another video, link in description)
3. One ask only - do not give five CTAs at the end

**Step 7: Write the thumbnail brief**

The thumbnail brief goes to the Creative Design Agent. Include:
- Main text (5 words max)
- Facial expression or reaction needed (if person is in thumbnail)
- Color direction
- What the thumbnail communicates without any text

## Output Format

```
YOUTUBE SCRIPT - [Client/Channel] - [Date]

VIDEO TITLE: [Title following the formula]
TARGET LENGTH: [X minutes]
TARGET KEYWORD: [What people search to find this]
AUDIENCE: [Who this is for]

---

THUMBNAIL BRIEF:
  Main text (5 words max): "[Text]"
  Visual concept: [What to show - person, graphic, before/after]
  Face expression: [If person appears: surprise, concern, excitement]
  Color: [Brand colors or high-contrast direction]
  Goal: [What the thumbnail communicates in 0.5 seconds]
  See thumbnail-brief.md for full format

---

SCRIPT:

[0:00-0:30] COLD OPEN
"[Word-for-word opening - result, problem, or bold claim first]"

[0:30-2:00] CREDENTIALS AND PREVIEW
"[Brief credentials + what the video covers]"

[2:00] CHAPTER 1: [Chapter name]
"[Word-for-word script]"

[RETENTION HOOK at ~4:00]
"[Retention device - open loop, pattern interrupt, or re-hook]"

[X:XX] CHAPTER 2: [Chapter name]
[Continue]

[RETENTION HOOK every 2-3 minutes]

[X:XX] CHAPTER [N]: [Chapter name]
[Continue]

[FINAL SECTION]
[CTA]
"[Summary + one ask]"

---

CHAPTER MARKERS (for YouTube description):
0:00 - [Chapter 1 name]
[X:XX] - [Chapter 2 name]
[Continue]

SCRIPT NOTES:
  Energy level: [High / Medium / Educational calm]
  On-camera or voiceover: [On camera / Voiceover + B-roll]
  B-roll needed: [Yes - see b-roll-plan.md / No]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Title follows the formula (specific result + timeframe or condition)
- [ ] Cold open does not start with "Hey guys welcome back"
- [ ] Credentials section is under 90 seconds
- [ ] Retention hook every 2-3 minutes
- [ ] End CTA has one ask, not multiple
- [ ] Thumbnail brief is included for Creative Design Agent
- [ ] Chapter markers are documented for YouTube description
- [ ] Script is word-for-word, not notes for the speaker to ad lib from
