# B-Roll Plan

## Purpose

Produce a specific shot list of B-roll footage needed for a video. B-roll is the secondary footage that plays over a voiceover or talking head. Generic B-roll directions ("show someone working on a laptop") produce generic-looking videos. Specific B-roll plans produce videos where the visuals reinforce the copy rather than just filling space.

## Used By

- Video Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (cross-references to ensure key spoken moments have visual support)

## Trigger

Use when scripting any video that will include B-roll (VSL, YouTube video, explainer, ad video). Can also be used when repurposing existing footage and needing to identify what to capture additionally.

## Inputs Needed

- Video script (what is being said in each section) - required
- Video type and tone (professional, candid, energetic, calm) - required
- Whether footage is being captured fresh or sourced from stock - required
- Any brand-specific visuals that must appear - helpful
- Budget or production capacity (informs whether to shoot fresh or use stock) - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Read the script and identify B-roll cue points**

Go through the script section by section. Mark every moment where:
- A specific object, place, or action is mentioned
- An abstract concept could be made concrete visually
- The viewer needs a visual break from talking head
- A result or outcome is described (show the result)
- A problem is described (show the problem)

**Step 2: Write specific B-roll descriptions**

For each cue point, write a specific description. Specific means:
- Who or what is in the shot
- What they are doing
- From what angle
- What mood or lighting
- What background or environment

Specific: "Business owner reviewing a dashboard showing rising lead numbers on a laptop screen, filmed over the shoulder from slightly above, bright modern office, positive expression"

Not specific: "Person looking at computer"

**Step 3: Categorize each shot**

| Category | What it is | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Proof shots | Screenshots, results, before/after | Requires actual client data |
| Product/service shots | The work being done or delivered | Brand-specific |
| Emotion shots | Reactions, expressions aligned with the narrative | Can be stock if client doesn't shoot |
| Environment shots | The space, tools, or setting | Stock or client location |
| Abstract concept shots | Visual metaphors for ideas | Stock usually works |

**Step 4: Assign source for each shot**

For each B-roll shot:
- **Client shoots**: Specific and brand-relevant shots that stock cannot replicate
- **Stock footage**: Generic situations that stock handles well (business environments, emotions, concepts)
- **Screen recording**: Dashboard screenshots, software demos, testimonial screenshots
- **Graphic/animation**: For data, frameworks, or stats that are better visualized as graphics

**Step 5: Match shots to script sections**

Build a table that maps each B-roll shot to the specific section of the script it supports.

## Output Format

```
B-ROLL PLAN - [Client/Video] - [Date]

VIDEO SCRIPT REF: [File name]
PRODUCTION TYPE: [Fresh shoot / Stock / Mixed]
TOTAL SECTIONS NEEDING B-ROLL: [N]

---

B-ROLL SHOT LIST:

| # | Script section | Script line reference | Shot description | Category | Source | Priority |
|---|--------------|----------------------|-----------------|---------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Hook | "Most businesses lose leads in the first hour" | Frustrated person looking at phone with missed calls notification, close-up, warm office light, concerned expression | Emotion | Stock | High |
| 2 | Problem section | "The follow-up system breaks down immediately" | Split screen: lead notification appearing → phone sitting unanswered → clock ticking → lead going cold | Concept | Animation | High |
| 3 | Proof section | "[Client name] went from 5 to 22 leads per month" | Screenshot of client's CRM dashboard showing lead numbers, zoomed to highlight the number, screen recording | Proof | Client provides | High |
| 4 | Mechanism section | "The automation handles it in seconds" | Screen recording of the workflow diagram animating, text appearing step by step | Product/concept | Screen recording | Medium |
| 5 | [Section] | "[Script line]" | [Specific description] | [Category] | [Source] | [Priority] |

---

SHOTS REQUIRING CLIENT INPUT:
These shots cannot be sourced from stock. Client must provide or arrange to film:
| # | Description | What client needs to provide |
|---|------------|---------------------------|
| 3 | CRM dashboard screenshot | Screenshot from their actual CRM with lead numbers |
| [N] | [Description] | [What is needed] |

---

STOCK FOOTAGE DIRECTION:
For shots sourced from stock, general direction for the stock search:
- Tone: [Bright and professional / Warm and candid / Dark and urgent]
- People to avoid: [Over-staged, fake smiling corporate stock]
- Preferred aesthetic: [Natural lighting, genuine expressions, real environments]

---

GRAPHICS NEEDED FROM CREATIVE DESIGN AGENT:
| # | Description | Appears in scene |
|---|------------|-----------------|
| 1 | [Animated stat or framework] | [Script section] |
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Every major claim or concept in the script has a B-roll cue
- [ ] Every B-roll description is specific (no "person at laptop" without full description)
- [ ] Each shot has a source identified (client, stock, or screen recording)
- [ ] High-priority shots are flagged so production can plan the most important ones first
- [ ] Shots requiring client involvement are listed separately so they can be requested early
- [ ] Graphics needed are flagged for Creative Design Agent
