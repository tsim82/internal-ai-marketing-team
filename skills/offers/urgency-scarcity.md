# Urgency and Scarcity

## Purpose

Design and document the legitimate urgency and scarcity elements for an offer. Urgency and scarcity are the reasons a buyer should act now rather than later. Fake urgency (countdown timers that reset, false "only 3 spots left" claims) damages trust and creates legal risk. This skill builds honest pressure that actually moves buyers.

## Used By

- Offer Agent (designs the mechanism)
- Copywriter (writes the urgency and scarcity copy)
- QA Agent (verifies claims before anything goes live)
- Funnel Agent (places urgency elements in the funnel)

## Trigger

Use when launching a new offer, when conversion rates are low and there is no clear reason to act now, or when a client asks "how do we get more people to say yes faster?"

## Inputs Needed

- Offer structure and delivery capacity (from offer buildout) - required
- Launch timeline or ongoing availability model - required
- Pricing and any promotional pricing planned - helpful
- Client's honest capacity (how many clients can they take on?) - required
- Legal constraints in the niche - required

## Process

**Step 1: Identify what is genuinely scarce or time-limited**

Before writing any urgency copy, document what is actually true. Fabricating urgency is a QA violation and a legal risk in most markets.

Real scarcity sources:
- **Capacity**: The client can only onboard 5 clients per month. This is real scarcity.
- **Cohort or launch**: The program only opens twice a year. This is real scarcity.
- **Pricing deadline**: The current price is a launch price that increases on a specific date. This is real urgency if the price actually increases.
- **Bonus deadline**: A specific bonus is only available before a deadline. This is real urgency if the bonus genuinely goes away.
- **External event**: A promotion tied to a real external date (end of tax year, end of fiscal quarter) that creates a natural reason to act.

If none of these exist honestly, do not fabricate them. Instead, find a real reason to act or advise the client to build one.

**Step 2: Document the specific parameters**

For each urgency or scarcity element, confirm:
- Is this actually true? (Can we verify it and would it hold up to scrutiny?)
- What is the deadline or limit?
- What happens to buyers who miss it?
- How is it tracked or enforced?

**Step 3: Decide where urgency appears in the funnel**

Urgency is most effective:
- Near the end of a VSL or webinar (right before the CTA)
- On the sales page close section
- In email sequences (Day 4-7 of a launch sequence or the follow-up sequence)
- On a "closing soon" email on the deadline day

Urgency introduced too early feels pushy. Urgency absent at the decision point leaves sales on the table.

**Step 4: Write the urgency and scarcity copy**

Two formats:

**Capacity-based (scarcity)**:
"We only take [N] new clients per month. This is not a sales tactic - it is because every client gets [specific personal attention]. When those spots are gone, the next available opening is [timeframe]. If you're serious about [outcome], the time to talk is now."

**Deadline-based (urgency)**:
"The current price of $[X] is available until [specific date/time]. After that, it goes to $[X]. This is a real increase - [reason for the increase]. If you want to lock in [offer name] at $[X], [CTA]."

Both versions must be factually true and verifiable. Do not write what cannot be honored.

**Step 5: Plan the scarcity or urgency countdown**

If a deadline is used:
- What is the exact date and time?
- What happens on the page when it expires (price automatically updates, spots counter decreases)?
- Who monitors the deadline enforcement?

Countdown timers that reset on page reload are a QA violation. Only use countdown technology that genuinely expires.

## Output Format

```
URGENCY AND SCARCITY - [Client/Offer] - [Date]

---

SCARCITY AUDIT:

Real scarcity available?
[ ] Capacity limit: [N] clients/cohort/month
[ ] Cohort/launch model: Opens [X times per year]
[ ] Other: [Description]

Real urgency available?
[ ] Pricing deadline: Current price $[X] increases to $[X] on [Date]
[ ] Bonus deadline: [Bonus name] available until [Date]
[ ] External event: [Event and date]
[ ] Other: [Description]

---

SELECTED MECHANISM(S):

Primary: [Capacity / Deadline / Bonus / External event]
Specific details: [What is true: how many spots, what date, what price change]
How it is enforced: [How the team tracks and honors this]
What happens if buyer misses it: [Specific - waitlist, price increase, next cohort]

Secondary (if applicable): [Same format]

---

FUNNEL PLACEMENT:

Where urgency appears:
[ ] VSL/webinar close section
[ ] Sales page close
[ ] Email sequence Day [N]
[ ] Deadline day email
[ ] Other: [Description]

---

URGENCY COPY (for Copywriter):

Scarcity line: "[Copy-ready sentence about scarcity]"
Urgency line: "[Copy-ready sentence about the deadline]"
Close paragraph: "[Full paragraph combining urgency, CTA, and what happens if they wait]"

---

QA CHECKLIST (complete before copy goes live):
[ ] Every scarcity claim is verifiable
[ ] Every deadline is a real, enforced deadline
[ ] Countdown timer (if used) does not reset on page reload
[ ] Price increase is real and will be honored
[ ] Capacity limit is real and tracked
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] All urgency and scarcity elements are verifiable and honest
- [ ] Fake countdown timers or fabricated "spots left" numbers are not used
- [ ] Enforcement mechanism is documented (how the team tracks capacity or deadline)
- [ ] QA Agent reviews all urgency claims before any copy goes live
- [ ] Copy explains what happens to buyers who miss the deadline (not just "act now")
