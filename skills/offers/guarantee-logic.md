# Guarantee Logic

## Purpose

Design a guarantee that lowers the buyer's perceived risk enough to act and is honest enough to hold up legally and operationally. A strong guarantee shifts the risk from the buyer to the seller. A weak guarantee is vague and does not move anyone. The output is the guarantee copy ready for the Copywriter to use in landing pages, VSLs, and sales conversations.

## Used By

- Offer Agent (designs the guarantee)
- Copywriter (writes the guarantee section in copy)
- QA Agent (flags any guarantee language that creates legal risk)
- Marketing Director (approves before use)

## Trigger

Use when building a new offer, when conversion rates are low and risk aversion may be a cause, or when the current guarantee is vague ("satisfaction guaranteed") and not moving buyers.

## Inputs Needed

- Completed offer buildout (what is being guaranteed) - required
- Delivery timeline from the offer buildout - required
- Refund or fulfillment capacity (can the team honor refunds?) - required
- Objection research, specifically risk-type objections - helpful
- Competitor guarantees for comparison - helpful
- Any legal constraints from the client or niche - required

## Process

**Step 1: Identify the top risk the buyer has**

From the objection research, what is the main thing the buyer is afraid of?

Common risk types:
- "It won't work for my situation" (specificity fear)
- "I'll pay and get nothing" (fulfillment fear)
- "I'll pay and the results won't be worth the price" (outcome fear)
- "I'll get locked in and can't leave" (commitment fear)

The guarantee should address the specific fear, not generic uncertainty.

**Step 2: Choose the guarantee type**

| Guarantee Type | What it says | Best for |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Outcome guarantee | "If you don't get [specific result] in [timeframe], we'll [make it right]" | Offers with measurable outcomes; high confidence in delivery |
| Satisfaction guarantee | "If you're not happy after [X], we'll refund you" | Lower-ticket; when outcome is harder to define |
| Performance guarantee | "We work for free until you hit [milestone]" | Service businesses with recurring revenue |
| Risk-reversal guarantee | "Try it for [X] days. If it's not what we promised, you get [refund / credit / extension]" | Mid-ticket; skeptical audiences |
| Done-for-you guarantee | "If we don't deliver [specific deliverable] in [timeframe], you get [specific remedy]" | Done-for-you services with clear deliverables |

**Step 3: Make the guarantee specific**

The guarantee must answer these questions:
- What specifically is being guaranteed?
- Over what timeframe?
- What happens if the guarantee is triggered (refund, extension, additional work)?
- What does the buyer need to do to qualify (e.g., must complete onboarding, attend calls)?
- What is NOT guaranteed?

Do not write a guarantee that is so conditional it is meaningless. If the conditions to qualify are so restrictive that almost no one qualifies, the guarantee does not lower risk.

**Step 4: Check the operational reality**

Can the business actually honor this guarantee?

Questions to check:
- If 10% of buyers triggered the guarantee, could refunds be paid?
- If fulfillment issues occur, is there capacity to do additional work?
- Does the guarantee timeline allow enough time for the offer to work?

A guarantee that cannot be honored is worse than no guarantee - it creates legal liability and destroys trust.

**Step 5: Write the guarantee copy**

The guarantee copy has four parts:
1. The guarantee name (something more specific than "Our Guarantee")
2. The guarantee statement (what is promised)
3. The conditions (what the buyer needs to do)
4. The action step (how to claim it)

## Output Format

```
GUARANTEE LOGIC - [Client/Offer] - [Date]

PRIMARY BUYER RISK: [The specific fear this guarantee addresses]

---

GUARANTEE TYPE: [Outcome / Satisfaction / Performance / Risk-reversal / Done-for-you]

---

GUARANTEE DETAILS:

What is guaranteed: [Specific, not vague]
Timeframe: [Days or weeks]
If triggered, buyer receives: [Specific remedy - refund amount, extension, free work]
Buyer qualifications to claim: [What they must have done to be eligible]
What is NOT covered: [Clear exclusions]

---

OPERATIONAL CHECK:
Can the business honor this? [Yes / Yes with conditions: [X] / No - see adjustment below]
If triggered by 10% of buyers, cost: $[X]
Fulfillment capacity if guarantee claims spike: [Note]

---

GUARANTEE COPY (for Copywriter):

GUARANTEE NAME: "[Name - e.g., The 90-Day Results Guarantee"]

GUARANTEE STATEMENT:
"[Full guarantee written in first or second person, conversational tone]"

CONDITIONS (written buyer-facing):
"To qualify, you need to [X]. If you complete [X] and do not see [result], simply [how to contact us] and we will [remedy]."

---

QA NOTE:
Phrases to avoid in this guarantee for legal reasons: [Any niche-specific restrictions]
Legal review needed before launch: [Yes / No / Only if making outcome claims in regulated niche]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Guarantee addresses the specific risk buyers have (not a generic "satisfaction" statement)
- [ ] Guarantee is specific: what is covered, for how long, and what happens
- [ ] Conditions are reasonable and do not eliminate almost everyone
- [ ] Operational reality is confirmed (can the team honor this?)
- [ ] Guarantee copy is written conversationally without hedging language
- [ ] QA Agent reviews before any outcome-based guarantees go to market

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
