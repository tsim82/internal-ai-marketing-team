# Offer Buildout

## Purpose

Build a complete, documented offer: what is included, how it is delivered, what the buyer gets and when, and how it is positioned. The offer buildout is the foundation every other agent works from. Copy, ads, funnels, and follow-up all reference the offer doc. Without a clear offer, the team cannot produce aligned work.

## Used By

- Offer Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (writes from the offer doc)
- Paid Ads Agent (derives ad angles from the offer)
- Funnel Agent (builds the funnel around the offer)
- CFO Agent (prices and analyzes profitability from this)

## Trigger

Use when launching a new offer, when an existing offer lacks documentation, when conversion rates are low and the suspected cause is offer clarity, or when the team cannot describe the offer in one sentence.

## Inputs Needed

- Service, product, or program description - required
- Target audience - required
- Main outcome promised - required
- Price (or price range being considered) - required
- Delivery format (done-for-you, coaching, course, software, physical product) - required
- Competitor research and offer analysis - helpful
- Client revenue goal - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Write the one-sentence offer summary**

Before anything else, write the offer in one sentence using this format:

"We help [specific audience] get [specific outcome] in [timeframe or method] without [thing they dread]."

If you cannot write this sentence with specifics, the offer is not clear enough to sell. Clarify before proceeding.

**Step 2: Define the core promise**

The core promise is the one transformation the buyer is paying for. Not a list of features. One specific, believable, desirable outcome.

Bad: "We provide comprehensive social media management services."
Good: "We take over your Instagram presence and get you 20+ qualified leads per month in 90 days."

The promise should be specific enough that both parties know whether it was delivered.

**Step 3: Build the delivery mechanism**

How does the promise get fulfilled? Document every step of delivery:

| Phase | What happens | Timeline | Who delivers |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Onboarding | [What] | [Week 1] | [Who] |
| Phase 1 | [What] | [Weeks 2-X] | [Who] |
| [Continue] | [...] | [...] | [...] |

This is for internal clarity and for the Copywriter (who needs to describe the mechanism in copy).

**Step 4: List the included deliverables**

Everything the buyer receives, listed specifically. Avoid vague bundle descriptions.

Bad: "Full marketing package"
Good: "3 emails per week, 5 social posts per week, monthly analytics report, bi-weekly strategy call"

**Step 5: Identify the upsell or backend**

What can the buyer buy next? What is the logical continuation of this offer? Document even if it does not exist yet - the Copywriter and CRM Follow-Up Agent need to know the buyer journey.

**Step 6: Identify the main risks to delivery**

What could prevent the outcome from being achieved? Be honest about dependencies (client must provide X, results depend on Y). This informs the guarantee and the promise wording.

## Output Format

```
OFFER BUILDOUT - [Client/Offer Name] - [Date]

ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY:
"We help [audience] get [outcome] in [timeframe/method] without [dread]."

---

OFFER NAME: [Name as it will appear in marketing]
PRICE: $[X] ([One-time / Monthly / Revenue share / etc.])
AUDIENCE: [Specific person this is for]

---

CORE PROMISE:
[One specific transformation. Write in buyer language, not service language.]

TIMEFRAME: [How long to see the promised outcome]

---

DELIVERY MECHANISM:

| Phase | What happens | Timeline | Delivered by |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| [Phase name] | [Description] | [Timing] | [Who] |

---

INCLUDED DELIVERABLES:
- [Specific deliverable]
- [Specific deliverable]
[Be specific - avoid vague bundle names]

---

ACCESS LEVEL / FORMAT:
[Done-for-you / Coaching / Course / Software / Hybrid]
[How client interacts with the team - calls, async, portal, etc.]

---

UPSELL / BACKEND OFFER:
[What happens after this offer. Even if not built yet, document the logical next step.]

---

DELIVERY RISKS / DEPENDENCIES:
- Client must provide: [X]
- Results depend on: [X]
- What is outside our control: [X]

---

PROOF NEEDED (for Copywriter):
- Case studies that match this offer: [List]
- Testimonials available: [Yes / No / Specific ones]
- Data points available: [List any specific stats or results to use]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] One-sentence summary uses a specific audience, outcome, timeframe, and dread element
- [ ] Core promise is specific enough to know whether it was delivered
- [ ] Delivery mechanism is documented phase by phase
- [ ] Deliverables are listed specifically (not "comprehensive package")
- [ ] Upsell or backend is noted
- [ ] Delivery dependencies are documented (not assumed)
- [ ] Proof available for the Copywriter is noted

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
