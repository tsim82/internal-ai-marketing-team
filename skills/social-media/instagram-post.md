# Instagram Post

## Purpose

Produce a complete, publish-ready Instagram post: caption with hook, body, and CTA, plus hashtag direction and visual brief. Instagram posts are short-to-medium length, visually led, and native to mobile. This skill applies to static image posts and carousel captions. Reel scripts use the short-form-video-script skill.

## Used By

- Social Media Agent (briefs the topic and assigns to Copywriter)
- Copywriter (writes the caption)
- Creative Design Agent (uses the visual brief to prep the image)
- QA Agent (reviews before publish)

## Trigger

Use when an Instagram post slot in the content calendar needs copy and a visual brief.

## Inputs Needed

- Content pillar and topic - required
- Goal of this post (educate, drive link click, social proof, engagement) - required
- Audience profile - required
- Post format (static or carousel) - required
- Brand voice rules - required
- Any specific proof points, stats, or stories to include - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Confirm format and goal**

- Static image post: caption-led, image supports the text
- Carousel post: first slide is the hook image, caption supports the swipe journey (see carousel-brief.md for carousel image direction)

Instagram caption optimal length: 50-150 words for most posts. Education posts can run longer (200-300 words) if the content is strong.

**Step 2: Write the opening line**

The first line is what appears before "more." It must stop the scroll.

The line must be one of:
- A direct hook (question, bold claim, or pain mirror)
- A result or number
- A provocative statement

Do not start with "Hi everyone" or "Today I want to share." That is not a hook.

**Step 3: Write the body**

After the hook, deliver on the promise of the opening line. For Instagram specifically:
- Short sentences
- One idea per paragraph
- Visual white space (each paragraph 1-2 lines max)
- No corporate language

**Step 4: Write the CTA**

Every Instagram post needs a CTA. Match it to the goal:
- Engagement: "Which one do you struggle with most? Tell me below."
- Link click: "Full breakdown link in bio."
- Lead gen: "DM me [word] and I'll send you [what]."
- Save: "Save this for your next [occasion]."

Avoid "Like and follow!" as a CTA.

**Step 5: Select hashtags**

Instagram hashtags: 3-5 in the caption, or move to first comment.

Hashtag strategy:
- 1-2 niche-specific hashtags (medium volume, 100k-500k posts)
- 1-2 community hashtags (your audience uses these)
- 0-1 brand hashtag (if client has one)

Do not use mega hashtags (10M+ posts). The content gets buried immediately.

**Step 6: Write the visual brief for Creative Design Agent**

One paragraph describing the image needed:
- What should be shown (person, object, scene, text overlay)
- Mood and tone
- Whether text overlay is needed and what it says
- Any brand color direction

## Output Format

```
INSTAGRAM POST - [Client] - [Date] - [Content Pillar]

FORMAT: [Static / Carousel]
GOAL: [Educate / Social proof / Engagement / Offer / Brand]
CONTENT CALENDAR SLOT: [Day and date]

---

CAPTION:

[Opening line - the hook]

[Body paragraph 1]

[Body paragraph 2 if needed]

[CTA]

[Hashtags]

---

WORD COUNT: [X]

---

VISUAL BRIEF FOR CREATIVE DESIGN AGENT:

Image concept: [What should be shown]
Mood/tone: [Warm, professional, candid, bold, etc.]
Text overlay needed: [Yes - "overlay text here" / No]
Brand colors: [Primary / Secondary / Brand kit reference]
Format: [1080x1080 square / 1080x1350 portrait]

---

NOTES:
[Any context for the Copywriter or designer - reference to a specific client result, quote, or event]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Opening line is a scroll-stopping hook, not a greeting
- [ ] Body has white space (short paragraphs, one idea each)
- [ ] CTA is specific to the post goal
- [ ] Hashtags are 3-5, relevant to the niche, not mega hashtags
- [ ] Visual brief is specific enough for the Creative Design Agent to execute without follow-up
- [ ] No em dashes, no banned words, no corporate tone
- [ ] Length is appropriate: 50-150 words for most posts, up to 300 for education content
