# Twitter / X Post

## Purpose

Write publish-ready Twitter/X posts: single tweets and threads. X rewards tight, opinionated writing more than any other platform. If the first tweet doesn't earn a click or a reply, the thread doesn't get read. This skill covers both single posts and multi-tweet threads for personal profiles and brand accounts.

## Used By

- Social Media Agent (briefs the topic and assigns)
- Copywriter (writes the post)
- QA Agent (reviews before publish)

## Trigger

Use when a Twitter/X slot in the content calendar needs copy, or when a thread is the right format for an idea that needs more than 280 characters.

## Inputs Needed

- Topic and content pillar - required
- Whether this is a single tweet or a thread - required
- Audience (who reads this account, what they care about) - required
- Goal (drive engagement, build authority, grow followers, push traffic) - required
- Brand voice rules - required
- Specific data points, takes, or proof to include - helpful

## Process

---

### Single Tweet

**Step 1: Know the format**

Twitter/X shows tweets in full with no "see more" truncation under 280 characters. Every character is visible. The writing has to earn the scroll-stop entirely on its own.

Character limits:
- Single tweet: 280 characters including spaces and punctuation
- With image: 280 characters (image counts as part of the card, not the text)
- With link: URL shortens to ~23 characters automatically - count it as 23

What earns engagement on X:
- Strong, specific opinions
- Short takes on something happening in the industry
- Counterintuitive facts or stats
- Personal observations with a specific detail
- One tight, memorable sentence

What doesn't work:
- Generic motivational content
- Vague corporate claims
- Posts that could apply to anyone in any industry
- Reposting LinkedIn posts verbatim

**Step 2: Write the tweet**

Write the tweet to do one of these things:
- Make someone stop and think
- Make someone want to reply or argue
- Make someone feel "that's exactly my situation"
- Make someone want to share it

Formats that consistently work:

| Format | Structure |
|--------|-----------|
| Hot take | "Unpopular opinion: [specific contrarian claim]" |
| Specific observation | "[Specific observation about how something actually works, with a detail that proves you've lived it]" |
| Stat + implication | "[Surprising stat]. Most people miss what this actually means: [the real takeaway]" |
| If/then | "If [audience] does [thing], the result is almost always [unexpected outcome]. Not because of [obvious reason]. Because of [real reason]." |
| Short story | "[One thing happened. Then this. Then this. What I learned:]" |
| Permission structure | "You don't need to [common expectation]. You need to [reframe]." |

**Step 3: Check the character count**

Write it out. Count manually or note the estimate. Stay under 280. If it's too long, cut the setup - the take is the tweet, not the context.

**Step 4: Hashtags**

1-2 maximum. X hashtags have lost most of their discovery value. Only use them if:
- The topic has a recognized community hashtag (e.g. #Founders, #MarketingTwitter)
- The client specifically wants to track the hashtag

Do not force hashtags in. A clean tweet without hashtags outperforms keyword-stuffed posts.

### Thread

**Step 1: Know when to use a thread**

Use a thread when:
- The idea needs more than 280 characters to land properly
- The content is a step-by-step process, a breakdown, or a list
- You want maximum visibility for a teaching post (threads get shared and saved more than single tweets)

Do not use a thread to pad thin content. A thread that could have been a single tweet will underperform.

**Step 2: Structure the thread**

Every thread follows the same core structure:

```
Tweet 1 (Hook): The entire thread lives or dies here. Must be self-contained enough to earn the click.
Tweet 2 (Proof or setup): Why this matters or why you know this
Tweets 3-N (Body): One idea per tweet. Numbered or flowing.
Final tweet (CTA): What to do next, follow, share, or reply
```

Tweet 1 hook rules:
- Under 220 characters (leave room for "🧵 Thread:" if using that format)
- Must work as a standalone tweet if someone doesn't click through
- Strong hook formats for threads:

| Hook Format | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Promise | "I've helped 30 service businesses fill their pipeline without cold outreach. Here's the system: [Thread]" |
| Counterintuitive | "Most marketing advice will slow your business down. Here's what actually moves the needle: [Thread]" |
| Story opener | "6 months ago we were at $0 in paid leads. Last month: 73 booked calls. What changed: [Thread]" |
| List preview | "7 things I wish I knew before running Meta ads: [Thread]" |

**Step 3: Write each tweet in the thread**

Rules per tweet:
- One idea per tweet. Do not combine two ideas.
- Keep each tweet under 240 characters so there's breathing room
- Vary length - a mix of short and medium tweets reads better than a wall of uniform blocks
- Number the tweets if it's a list format (1/, 2/, 3/ etc.)
- Do not end a tweet mid-sentence to force the click to the next one - readers find this annoying

**Step 4: Write the closing tweet**

The last tweet should:
- Summarize the main takeaway in one line
- Give a clear CTA: "Follow @[handle] for more posts like this" or "Retweet the first tweet if this was useful" or "Reply with your biggest takeaway"
- Not introduce a new idea

## Output Format

### Single Tweet

```
X/TWITTER POST - [Client / Profile name] - [Date] - [Content Pillar]

ACCOUNT TYPE: [Personal / Brand]
GOAL: [Engagement / Authority / Traffic / Follower growth]
FORMAT: Single tweet

---

TWEET:

[Tweet text - max 280 characters]

---

CHARACTER COUNT: [X / 280]
HASHTAGS: [List if used, or "None"]
VISUAL: [Image needed - describe / GIF / No visual]

NOTES:
[Any context about timing, what this connects to, or what makes it work]
```

---

### Thread

```
X/TWITTER THREAD - [Client / Profile name] - [Date] - [Content Pillar]

ACCOUNT TYPE: [Personal / Brand]
GOAL: [Engagement / Authority / Traffic / Follower growth]
FORMAT: Thread ([X] tweets)

---

TWEET 1 (HOOK):
[Hook tweet text - under 220 chars ideally]

---

TWEET 2:
[Text]

---

TWEET 3:
[Text]

[Continue for all tweets]

---

FINAL TWEET (CTA):
[Closing tweet with follow, share, or reply CTA]

---

THREAD TOTALS:
Total tweets: [X]
Estimated read time: [X minutes at ~30 seconds per tweet]

VISUAL NOTES:
[Any images, screenshots, or graphics to include and which tweet they attach to]

NOTES:
[Context on the thread strategy, what angle is being tested, or what inspired this topic]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Single tweet is under 280 characters
- [ ] Hook tweet (for threads) works as a standalone post
- [ ] One idea per tweet - no cramming
- [ ] No corporate language, no hype words
- [ ] Hashtags: 0-2 max
- [ ] Final tweet has a clear CTA
- [ ] No em dashes anywhere
- [ ] Reads like a real person wrote it, not a brand account
- [ ] Thread tweets are numbered if it's a list format
