# LinkedIn Post

## Purpose

Produce a complete, publish-ready LinkedIn post: copy with hook, structured body, and CTA. LinkedIn has the highest organic reach of any major social platform for B2B content when the post is genuinely useful and professionally relevant. This skill covers organic LinkedIn posts for personal profiles or company pages.

## Used By

- Social Media Agent (briefs the topic and assigns)
- Copywriter (writes the post)
- QA Agent (reviews before publish)

## Trigger

Use when a LinkedIn post slot in the content calendar needs copy.

## Inputs Needed

- Content pillar and topic - required
- Whether this is for a personal profile or company page - required
- Audience (job titles, industries, roles the content is for) - required
- Goal (educate, social proof, drive DMs, establish authority) - required
- Brand voice rules - required
- Any specific proof points, experience, or results to reference - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Understand LinkedIn content behavior**

LinkedIn favors:
- Personal voice (even on company pages, first-person outperforms third-person)
- Genuine professional insight over polished corporate content
- Posts that generate comments (LinkedIn's algorithm weights comments heavily)
- Carousels (document posts) for educational content
- Native video for personal brand content

LinkedIn users scroll in a professional mindset. They want to learn, get credibility signals, and make connections.

**Step 2: Write the hook line (critical)**

LinkedIn shows only the first 2-3 lines before "...see more." The opening line must earn the read.

Strong LinkedIn hook formats:
- Contrarian take: "Everyone says you need more leads. Your problem is probably something else."
- Specific result: "I helped 17 service businesses cut their CPL in half. The fix was the same every time."
- Confession: "I was wrong about [common belief] for 3 years. Here's what I missed."
- Question: "What's the real reason your pipeline is unpredictable?"

The hook cannot be generic. It must speak directly to the professional the post is for.

**Step 3: Write the body with high white space**

LinkedIn is read on desktop and mobile, often in quick scrolls. Format aggressively for readability:
- One idea per line
- Double return between ideas
- 3-5 word maximum per line for key statements
- Never 3+ consecutive sentences in a row without a line break

Optimal length: 150-300 words for standard posts. 300-600 for teaching posts with a strong structure.

Structure options:
- **List structure**: Hook → 3-5 short insight bullets → CTA
- **Story arc**: Situation → Problem → Action → Result → Takeaway
- **Framework**: Hook → Name the framework → 3 steps → Apply to reader's situation → CTA

**Step 4: Write the CTA**

LinkedIn CTAs that work:
- "What's your experience with this? Drop it in the comments."
- "DM me if you're dealing with this - happy to share what we did."
- "Repost this if someone on your team needs to see it."
- "Follow for more posts like this every [frequency]."

Do not end LinkedIn posts without a CTA. Posts that end abruptly get scrolled past without engagement.

**Step 5: Hashtags**

0-3 hashtags at the end of the post. LinkedIn hashtags have less impact than on other platforms but help with discovery:
- 1 niche hashtag
- 1 topic hashtag
- 1 broad professional hashtag (optional)

## Output Format

```
LINKEDIN POST - [Client / Profile name] - [Date] - [Content Pillar]

ACCOUNT TYPE: [Personal profile / Company page]
GOAL: [Educate / Social proof / Authority / Drive DMs / Engagement]
AUDIENCE: [Job titles or audience the post is written for]
CONTENT CALENDAR SLOT: [Day and date]

---

POST TEXT:

[Hook line - 1 strong sentence]

[Line 2 if needed to set up the hook - optional]

[Body - formatted with heavy line breaks]

[CTA]

#[Hashtag1] #[Hashtag2] #[Hashtag3]

---

WORD COUNT: [X]

---

VISUAL (if applicable):
For document/carousel post: see carousel-brief.md
For image: [Visual brief - image concept, text overlay, format 1200x627]
For text-only post: [No visual needed]

---

NOTES:
[Any personal context, results, or experience the Copywriter should weave in to make the post feel authentic to the client]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Opening line earns the "see more" before any scroll
- [ ] Heavy white space throughout (one idea per line, breaks between)
- [ ] CTA is specific and at the end
- [ ] First-person voice even if for a company page
- [ ] No corporate language, no AI hype words
- [ ] 0-3 hashtags only
- [ ] Length: 150-300 words for standard, up to 600 for teaching posts
- [ ] No em dashes
