# Short-Form Caption

## Purpose

Write the caption for a short-form video post (TikTok, Instagram Reel, YouTube Short, or Facebook Reel). The caption for short-form video is short, supports the video content, and drives an action. It is not a standalone copy piece - it works alongside the video, not instead of it.

## Used By

- Social Media Agent (briefs the topic)
- Copywriter (writes the caption)
- Video Agent (pairs with the short-form video script)
- QA Agent (reviews before publish)

## Trigger

Use when a short-form video has been scripted or produced and needs a publish-ready caption.

## Inputs Needed

- Short-form video script or summary of the video content - required
- Platform (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) - required
- Goal (educate, drive profile visit, generate comments, lead gen via DM) - required
- Brand voice rules - required
- Any key phrases or hooks from the video script - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Understand the caption's role for short-form**

On short-form platforms, most users watch the video first and read the caption second, or not at all. The caption should:
1. Reinforce what the video was about (for SEO and discoverability)
2. Give a reason to take action after watching
3. Ask a question to drive comments

The caption does not need to re-explain the video. It extends or deepens it.

**Step 2: Write the opening line**

The opening line of the caption appears below the video as a preview. It should either:
- Pull from the hook of the video ("Here's the thing nobody talks about with lead gen...")
- Tease what they just watched ("Watch this if your pipeline feels random")
- Ask a direct question ("Are you making this mistake?")

**Step 3: Write the caption body**

Short-form captions: 50-100 words maximum. On TikTok, shorter is better (the video is the content).

What to include:
- 1-2 sentences reinforcing the main takeaway of the video
- A question or call to comment
- CTA (DM, follow, link in bio if applicable)

What to skip:
- Recapping the video in the caption (they just watched it)
- Long explanations
- Multiple competing CTAs

**Step 4: Hashtags**

| Platform | Hashtag approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| TikTok | 3-5 hashtags: 1-2 niche + 1-2 content topic + 1 trend or FYP tag if relevant |
| Instagram Reels | 3-5 hashtags, same logic as Instagram posts |
| YouTube Shorts | No hashtags needed; discovery is algorithm-driven, not hashtag-driven |
| Facebook Reels | 0-2 hashtags or none |

## Output Format

```
SHORT-FORM CAPTION - [Client] - [Platform] - [Date]

VIDEO TOPIC: [Brief description of what the video covers]
GOAL: [Comments / DMs / Profile visit / Education]
CONTENT CALENDAR SLOT: [Day and date]

---

CAPTION:

[Opening line - pulls from video hook or teases the takeaway]

[1-2 sentence reinforcement or question]

[CTA]

[Hashtags]

---

WORD COUNT: [X - should be 50-100]

---

NOTES:
[Any context - is this part of a series? Does the video have a spoken CTA the caption should reinforce?]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Caption is 50-100 words, not a re-explanation of the video
- [ ] Opening line works as a scroll-stopper below the video thumbnail
- [ ] Includes a comment-driving question or clear CTA
- [ ] Hashtag count matches platform norms
- [ ] No em dashes, no corporate language
- [ ] Caption extends the video rather than duplicating it

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
