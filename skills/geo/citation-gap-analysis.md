# Citation Gap Analysis

## Purpose

Identify where a brand is missing from the directories, publications, podcasts, roundups, and community mentions that AI tools use as citation sources. Competitors that appear in AI responses usually have citations from these sources. The citation gap analysis finds the specific gaps and produces a prioritized list of citation opportunities to pursue.

## Used By

- GEO Agent (primary)
- Marketing Director (receives the opportunity list)

## Trigger

Use as part of a GEO audit after the entity audit, or when the AI Visibility Snapshot shows competitors being cited in AI responses but the brand is not.

## Inputs Needed

- Brand name - required
- 3-5 competitors to benchmark against - required
- Niche and geography (local or national) - required
- AI Visibility Snapshot results (which sources did AI tools cite?) - required
- Any existing brand mentions online - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Extract citations from AI responses**

Review the AI Visibility Snapshot. When a competitor or niche term appeared in AI responses, what did the AI cite as sources?

Perplexity and Google AI Overviews show citations directly. For ChatGPT and Claude, read the response carefully - they often reference the type of source ("according to industry analysts" or "featured in [publication type]") even if not linking.

List every source category mentioned across the AI responses.

**Step 2: Build the citation benchmark**

For each competitor that appeared prominently in AI responses, research where they have been cited online:

| Citation type | Competitor 1 | Competitor 2 | Our brand | Gap |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Industry directory listings | [List] | [List] | [List] | [Missing: X, Y] |
| Niche media features/mentions | [List] | [List] | [List] | [...] |
| Podcast appearances | [N] | [N] | [N] | [...] |
| Guest articles / contributed content | [List] | [List] | [List] | [...] |
| Review platform listings | [List] | [List] | [List] | [...] |
| Community mentions (Reddit, FB groups) | [High / Low] | [...] | [...] | [...] |

**Step 3: Organize citations into three tiers**

**Tier 1 - High impact (pursue immediately):**
- Relevant industry directories (Clutch, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or niche-specific)
- Top niche media features or roundup articles ("Best [X] agencies in [niche]")
- Authoritative review platforms relevant to the niche

**Tier 2 - Medium impact (pursue within 90 days):**
- Industry podcasts (guest appearances)
- Guest articles in niche publications
- Contributed content in industry blogs with authority
- Voice of customer content (case studies with external publication)

**Tier 3 - Long-tail (ongoing):**
- Community participation (Reddit, LinkedIn, Facebook Groups)
- Social proof accumulation (testimonials published externally)
- Local directory listings (for local service businesses)

**Step 4: Identify specific targets in each tier**

For Tier 1, name the exact directory, platform, or publication and confirm:
- Is this platform a real citation source for AI in this niche?
- Does the brand qualify to be listed?
- What is the process (free listing, paid placement, editorial)?

**Step 5: Prioritize by effort-to-impact ratio**

Some citations take 10 minutes to create (directory listing). Others take weeks (niche publication feature). Prioritize:
- Fast + high impact: free directory listings where competitors appear
- Moderate effort + high impact: podcast pitches, guest article outreach
- High effort + high impact: press features, Wikipedia notability (only if achievable)
