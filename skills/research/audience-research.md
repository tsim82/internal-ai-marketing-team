# Audience Research

## Purpose

Build a detailed psychographic and demographic profile of the target audience. Goes deeper than surface demographics to capture beliefs, fears, desires, prior experiences, and decision-making patterns. The output is what the Copywriter and Paid Ads Agent use to write and target with precision.

## Used By

- Market Research Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (tone, language, and angle calibration)
- Paid Ads Agent (audience targeting and creative direction)
- Offer Agent (positioning and framing)

## Trigger

Use when starting a new client engagement, when targeting a new audience segment, or when copy or ads feel generic and the team suspects a shallow audience understanding is the cause.

## Inputs Needed

- Target audience description from client - required
- Niche or product category - required
- Existing customer data, reviews, or testimonials - helpful
- Sales call recordings or notes (if available) - helpful
- Competitor audience signals (from competitor research) - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Confirm who the buyer actually is vs. who the client thinks they are**
Clients often have an idealized picture of their audience. The research should confirm or correct this. Common mismatches: client says "business owners 25-55" but actual buyers are women in their 30s-40s running service businesses. Specificity beats breadth.

**Step 2: Build the demographic snapshot**
Pull what is documentable from reviews, testimonials, and any available data:
- Age range (not a guess - pull from actual reviews or client data)
- Gender split (if relevant)
- Business type or industry (if B2B)
- Geography (local, national, or global)
- Income or revenue range (where documentable)

**Step 3: Map the psychographic profile**
This is the core of the research. Pull from reviews, forums, social comments, and interviews if available.

Profile six dimensions:
1. **Current situation**: What does their day-to-day look like right now? What problem are they sitting with?
2. **Desired outcome**: What does "success" look like to them? Be specific. Not "grow my business" - "stop working weekends and have my pipeline full."
3. **Prior attempts**: What have they already tried to solve this problem? What happened? Why did it not work?
4. **Core fears**: What are they afraid will happen if they buy and it does not work? What are they afraid others will think?
5. **Decision triggers**: What finally makes them act? What would need to be true for them to buy today?
6. **Identity**: How do they see themselves? What do they believe about people like them that might block or accelerate a purchase?

**Step 4: Find the emotional language**
The goal is to find the exact words buyers use when describing their own situation. Not a paraphrase - the actual words.

Sources ranked by quality:
1. Sales call transcripts or notes (highest quality - unprompted language)
2. Customer testimonials (their own words after buying)
3. 1-star and 2-star reviews of competitors (what they say when frustrated)
4. 5-star reviews of competitors (what they say when it works)
5. Reddit and Facebook Group threads where they discuss the problem
6. YouTube comments on relevant videos

Pull 15-20 direct quotes minimum. Label each with its source.

**Step 5: Identify the buying window**
When do people in this market buy? What event or situation triggers them to look for a solution? Examples:
- "After losing a client I knew I needed a better pipeline system"
- "When I hit $10k/month I realized I couldn't keep doing everything manually"
- "My competitor started running ads and I started losing market share"

The buying window is what the Paid Ads Agent uses for targeting signals and what the Copywriter uses to open copy.

**Step 6: Note what they are NOT**
What this audience is not helps as much as what they are. Negative targeting signals: what prior experience do they come in with that might make them skeptical? What do they assume about solutions in this space that is wrong?

## Output Format

```
AUDIENCE RESEARCH - [Client/Offer] - [Date]

TARGET AUDIENCE LABEL: [Short description - e.g., "Service business owners stuck at $10-30k/mo"]

---

DEMOGRAPHIC SNAPSHOT:
Age range: [X-X, based on: source]
Gender: [Split if relevant]
Business type: [If B2B]
Revenue/income: [Range if documentable]
Geography: [Local / National / Global]

---

PSYCHOGRAPHIC PROFILE:

1. Current situation:
"[In their words or close to it: what is their day-to-day like right now?]"

2. Desired outcome:
"[Specific: what does winning look like to them?]"

3. Prior attempts and failures:
  - Tried: [X] - Result: [What happened and why it failed for them]
  - Tried: [Y] - Result: [...]
  - Pattern: [What keeps failing and why]

4. Core fears:
  - Biggest fear: "[What they are afraid of]"
  - Secondary fear: "[...]"

5. Decision triggers:
  - "[What would need to be true for them to buy?]"
  - "[What event or moment triggers them to look for help?]"

6. Identity:
  - How they see themselves: "[...]"
  - Belief that blocks purchase: "[...]"
  - Belief that accelerates purchase: "[...]"

---

EMOTIONAL LANGUAGE (raw quotes):
- "[Direct quote]" - Source: [sales call / review / Reddit / etc.]
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]
[15-20 quotes minimum]

---

BUYING WINDOW:
Trigger event: "[What happens that makes them look for a solution?]"
Timing: [How long they typically look before buying]
What tips them: [The thing that turns a looker into a buyer]

---

WHAT THEY ARE NOT:
- "[Common misconception the team should not make about this audience]"
- "[Prior experience that makes them skeptical of X type of claims]"
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] All six psychographic dimensions are filled in with specifics, not generalizations
- [ ] At least 15 direct quotes from real sources
- [ ] Each quote has its source labeled
- [ ] Buying window is documented with a trigger event
- [ ] Prior attempts include what failed and why (not just that they tried)
- [ ] Output is specific enough for the Copywriter to write in this audience's voice

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
