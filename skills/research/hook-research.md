# Hook Research

## Purpose

Produce a set of tested and sourced hook angles for a specific market, offer, or campaign. A hook is the opening line that stops someone from scrolling or clicking away. Hook research finds the emotional triggers, specific frustrations, and curiosity gaps that the Copywriter and Paid Ads Agent can use to open ads, emails, social posts, and VSLs.

## Used By

- Market Research Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (direct input for all copy openings)
- Paid Ads Agent (creative angle direction)
- Social Media Agent (first-line hooks for social posts)

## Trigger

Use when launching a new campaign, when creative is fatiguing and new angles are needed, when hooks feel generic, or when copy open rates and CTRs are below target.

## Inputs Needed

- Audience profile (from audience research) - required
- Competitor ad hooks (from competitor research) - helpful
- Any existing copy that has performed well (to understand what already resonated) - helpful
- Market sophistication level (from market research brief) - required
- Niche or product category - required

## Process

**Step 1: Source raw emotional language**
Before writing any hooks, collect the source material. Pull from:

| Source | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| Amazon reviews (niche books) | Phrases describing frustration, the moment they realized they needed help, what they tried |
| Reddit threads (/r/[niche], /r/entrepreneur, etc.) | Rants, questions, "I've tried everything" posts |
| Facebook Group posts | Public complaints, questions asking for advice |
| YouTube comments on competitor videos | Frustrated viewers, people who tried the solution and report back |
| 1-star reviews of competitors | Exact words used to describe what went wrong |
| Sales call notes or transcripts | Unprompted phrases from prospects |
| Competitor ad comments | Both positive ("this is exactly what I needed") and negative ("I tried this and...") |

**Step 2: Extract the emotional core**
From the raw language, identify the patterns. What words or phrases appear over and over? What emotions are strongest: frustration, fear, embarrassment, hope, urgency? What specific situations keep being mentioned?

Group the raw quotes into emotional themes:
- Frustration/stuck: phrases about feeling like they are spinning their wheels
- Fear/risk: phrases about what they are afraid will happen
- Aspiration: phrases about what they want to achieve or who they want to be
- Skepticism: phrases about what has not worked and why they might not trust this
- Urgency: phrases about the cost of not solving this now

**Step 3: Derive the hooks**
From each emotional theme, write 2-3 hook options. Use the six hook types:

| Hook Type | Structure | Best for |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Curiosity gap | "[Something is wrong with X] and it's not what you think" | Cold traffic, Level 3-4 awareness |
| Pain mirror | "You've probably [specific frustrating experience]..." | Cold traffic, Level 2-3 awareness |
| Proof/result | "We helped [specific person] go from [A] to [B] in [time]" | Warm traffic, Level 4-5 awareness |
| Contrarian | "Stop [commonly recommended thing] if you want to [goal]" | Any warm traffic, creates pattern interrupt |
| Bold claim | "[Specific claim that challenges their current belief]" | Works when the mechanism is genuinely different |
| Direct benefit | "[Specific result] without [thing they dread]" | Retargeting and warm audiences |

**Step 4: Differentiate from competitor hooks**
Pull the hooks competitors are already using. Any hook this team uses should not sound like the market. If every competitor opens with "Are you struggling to get clients?", the team should not use that same opening.

Mark which hooks overlap with competitors and deprioritize them.

**Step 5: Prioritize by emotional intensity and specificity**
The best hooks are:
- Specific (name the situation, not a vague category)
- Emotionally resonant (hit a real feeling, not a general problem)
- Different from what the audience has already heard
- Appropriate to the awareness level of the traffic

Rank the hooks and label the top 5 as recommended for testing first.

```
HOOK RESEARCH - [Client/Offer/Campaign] - [Date]

MARKET SOPHISTICATION LEVEL: [1-5]
AUDIENCE AWARENESS LEVEL: [Cold / Warm / Retargeting]

---

RAW SOURCE MATERIAL:

Frustration/stuck theme:
- "[Quote]" - Source: [Amazon review / Reddit / etc.]
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]

Fear/risk theme:
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]

Aspiration theme:
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]

Skepticism theme:
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]

Urgency/cost-of-inaction theme:
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]

---

DERIVED HOOKS (ranked by recommended testing priority):

TOP 5 RECOMMENDED HOOKS:

1. Hook: "[Full hook line]"
   Type: [Curiosity gap / Pain mirror / Proof / Contrarian / Bold / Direct benefit]
   Derived from: "[Quote or pattern it comes from]"
   Best for: [Format or audience type]

2. Hook: "[Full hook line]"
   [Same format]

3. [...]
4. [...]
5. [...]

ADDITIONAL HOOKS (lower priority / test after top 5):

6. "[Hook]"
7. "[Hook]"
[Etc.]

---

COMPETITOR HOOKS TO AVOID (already saturated in the market):
- "[Hook or opening competitor is using]" - Used by: [Competitor]
- [...]

---

NOTES FOR COPYWRITER:
- Strongest emotional theme in this market: [theme]
- Language patterns to use: [specific phrases from research]
- Language to avoid: [phrases that feel generic for this niche]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] At least 5 raw quotes per emotional theme with sources labeled
- [ ] At minimum 10 derived hook options with hook type labeled
- [ ] Top 5 ranked with rationale
- [ ] Competitor hooks documented and deprioritized
- [ ] All hooks are specific to this market, not generic
- [ ] Sophistication and awareness level noted for each hook's recommended use

Phase 3 complete.
