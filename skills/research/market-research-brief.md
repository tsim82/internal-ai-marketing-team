# Market Research Brief

## Purpose

Produce a complete market research brief for a new client, offer, or campaign. The brief becomes the foundational document that the Copywriter, Offer Agent, Paid Ads Agent, and Funnel Agent all draw from. If this brief is weak, every downstream output is weaker.

## Used By

- Market Research Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (pulls buyer language, awareness level, and hooks)
- Offer Agent (pulls JTBD, objections, and market sophistication level)
- Paid Ads Agent (pulls audience targeting inputs)
- Marketing Director (assigns when starting a new project)

## Trigger

Use at the start of any new client engagement, new offer launch, or when entering a new market. Also use when the team is producing work that feels generic or when copy is not converting.

## Inputs Needed

- Client name and website - required
- Offer name and description - required
- Known target audience (who the client thinks they are selling to) - required
- Main competitors (minimum 3) - required
- Access to any existing customer data, reviews, or testimonials - helpful
- Prior research if any has been done - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Define the market**
Write a one-paragraph description of the market: the type of person, the situation they are in, and the problem they are experiencing. This is not the brand's target customer statement - it is a neutral description of real humans in this market.

**Step 2: Identify the Jobs to Be Done**
For this offer and market, identify:
- Functional job: what task does the buyer want to get done? (The practical thing)
- Emotional job: how do they want to feel when it is done?
- Social job: how do they want others to perceive them after the purchase?

Write each as: "When [situation], I want to [job to be done], so I can [outcome or feeling]."

**Step 3: Determine the market sophistication level**
Use the 5-level scale:
- Level 1: Problem-unaware. Does not know this problem exists or see it as a problem.
- Level 2: Problem-aware. Knows the problem but does not know solutions exist.
- Level 3: Solution-aware. Knows solutions exist but is evaluating options.
- Level 4: Product-aware. Knows the category but has not chosen you.
- Level 5: Most aware. Knows the brand, may have seen marketing before.

State the level clearly and why. This determines how the Copywriter opens all copy.

**Step 4: Identify the top 5 objections**
Pull objections from reviews, competitor comments, forums, and customer conversations. Categorize each:
- Skepticism: "I'm not sure it will work for me"
- Specificity: "I'm not sure it will work for my situation"
- Experience: "I've tried things like this before"
- Financial: "I can't afford it right now"
- Timing: "This isn't the right time"

For each: state it, rate severity (1-3 where 3 is most common), note where it came from.

**Step 5: Mine buyer language (raw quotes)**
Pull 10-20 phrases directly from the market. These are words real buyers use to describe their problem, goal, or frustration. Sources: Amazon book reviews in the niche, Reddit threads, Facebook Group comments, G2 reviews, YouTube comments, Trustpilot.

Format as raw quotes, not paraphrases. The Copywriter needs the actual words.

**Step 6: Map the competitor landscape**
For top 3-5 competitors:
- What is their primary claim or promise?
- Who are they targeting?
- What angle do they lead with in ads?
- What do customers complain about (from reviews)?
- What do customers praise (from reviews)?

This surfaces whitespace: what the market is not being told, and what competitors are failing to deliver.

**Step 7: Determine brand awareness in the market**
Has the target audience seen this brand's ads? Any existing perception (positive or negative)? This determines how much credibility work the Copywriter needs in the opening.

**Step 8: Write the summary section**
5-7 sentences covering: who the market is, what they want, what is stopping them, what sophistication level they are at, and what the biggest opportunity is. This goes at the top. Write it last.

## Output Format

```
MARKET RESEARCH BRIEF - [Offer/Client] - [Date]

SUMMARY (write last, place first):
[5-7 sentence overview]

---

SECTION 1: MARKET DEFINITION
[One paragraph]

---

SECTION 2: JOBS TO BE DONE
Functional job: "When [X], I want to [Y], so I can [Z]"
Emotional job: "When [X], I want to feel [Y], so I can [Z]"
Social job: "When [X], I want others to see me as [Y]"

---

SECTION 3: MARKET SOPHISTICATION LEVEL
Level: [1-5]
Rationale: [Why - what evidence]
Implication for copy: [How the Copywriter should open all copy for this market]

---

SECTION 4: OBJECTION MAP
| Objection | Type | Severity (1-3) | Source |
|-----------|------|----------------|--------|
| "[Objection]" | [Type] | [Score] | [Where found] |
[Top 5 minimum]

---

SECTION 5: BUYER LANGUAGE (raw quotes)
- "[Direct quote from real buyer]" - Source: [Amazon / Reddit / Review]
- "[Quote]" - Source: [...]
[10 quotes minimum]

---

SECTION 6: COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE
Competitor 1: [Name]
  Primary claim: "[what they promise]"
  Target audience: [who]
  Ad angle: [what they lead with]
  Price: $[X]
  Customer complaints: [from reviews]
  Customer praise: [from reviews]
[Repeat for top 3-5]

Whitespace opportunity: [What the market is not being told / what competitors fail to deliver]

---

SECTION 7: BRAND AWARENESS IN MARKET
[How aware is the target audience of this brand?]
Implication for copy: [How much credibility work the Copywriter needs to do]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Jobs to Be Done written as "When / want to / so I can" statements
- [ ] Sophistication level stated with rationale
- [ ] Objection map has at least 5 objections with severity scores
- [ ] Buyer language has at least 10 direct quotes with sources
- [ ] Competitor landscape covers at least 3 competitors
- [ ] Whitespace opportunity explicitly named
- [ ] Summary written last, placed first
- [ ] Specific enough that the Copywriter can write a first draft without additional research
