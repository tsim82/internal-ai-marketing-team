# Objection Research

## Purpose

Identify and document every meaningful objection a prospect in this market has to buying. Map objections by type, severity, and source. The output is what the Copywriter uses to answer objections in copy, what the CRM Follow-Up Agent uses to train sales conversations, and what the Offer Agent uses to design guarantees and positioning.

## Used By

- Market Research Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (handles objections in VSL, landing page, and email copy)
- CRM Follow-Up Agent (trains sales conversations)
- Offer Agent (designs guarantees and removes objection barriers)

## Trigger

Use when starting a new campaign, when close rates are lower than expected, when prospects are asking the same questions repeatedly, or when sales calls stall at the same point.

## Inputs Needed

- Sales call notes or recordings (if available) - highest quality input
- Competitor reviews (especially 2-3 star reviews) - required
- Forum and community posts from the niche - required
- Audience profile (from audience research) - helpful
- Any objections the client or sales team has observed - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Collect raw objections from every available source**

Ranked by quality:
1. Sales call notes: "They said they needed to think about it" or "They mentioned they tried something similar before"
2. Direct messages or email replies to ads/emails: "Is this really different from X?"
3. Competitor 1-5 star reviews: what made people hesitate, what complaints came up before purchase, what wish-they-knew information appears
4. Reddit and Facebook Groups: "Has anyone tried [category]? I'm worried about..."
5. Comment sections on competitor ads: questions and concerns people post publicly

Write each objection down as the prospect would say it, not as a category name.

**Step 2: Categorize each objection**

| Category | What It Means | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Skepticism** | They don't believe the claim or the result | "I've heard promises like this before and it didn't work" |
| **Specificity** | They believe it works but don't think it applies to them | "That might work for [other type], but my situation is different because..." |
| **Experience** | They tried something similar and it failed | "I paid for [X] and it was a waste of money" |
| **Financial** | Price is a barrier | "I can't afford it right now" or "It's too expensive" |
| **Timing** | Not ready to commit now | "This isn't the right time" or "Maybe in a few months" |
| **Authority** | They don't trust who is selling | "I don't know enough about you/your company" |
| **Risk** | They're afraid of making a wrong decision | "What if it doesn't work?" or "What's your refund policy?" |

**Step 3: Score each objection by severity**

Severity 3 - Kills the sale if not addressed. This objection will stop most prospects from buying.
Severity 2 - Comes up frequently but can be overcome with the right response.
Severity 1 - Minor hesitation, usually addressed by the guarantee or proof alone.

Mark each objection with its severity.

**Step 4: Find the real objection behind the stated objection**
Many objections are surface expressions of a deeper concern. Examples:
- "I can't afford it" often means "I'm not convinced it will work enough to justify the risk"
- "I need to think about it" often means "I'm not convinced yet but don't want to say no"
- "The timing isn't right" often means "I don't feel enough urgency to act now"

For severity 2 and 3 objections, note what the real objection is likely to be.

**Step 5: Map objection to copy or conversation response**
For each severity 2-3 objection, write a one-sentence direction for how the Copywriter or sales team should handle it:
- Where in the copy it should be addressed (FAQ, guarantee section, proof block)
- What type of evidence answers it best (testimonial, case study, risk reversal, mechanism explanation)

## Output Format

```
OBJECTION RESEARCH - [Client/Offer] - [Date]

SALES CALL NOTES AVAILABLE: [Yes / No]
SOURCE QUALITY NOTE: [Brief comment on data quality - "based primarily on reviews, no call data available"]

---

OBJECTION MAP:

| # | Objection (as stated by prospect) | Category | Severity | Real objection | Source |
|---|----------------------------------|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | "[Exact words]" | [Type] | [1-2-3] | "[Deeper concern]" | [Source] |
| 2 | "[...]" | [...] | [...] | [...] | [...] |
[Minimum 8 objections]

---

SEVERITY 3 OBJECTIONS (must address in copy):

Objection: "[Exact wording]"
Real concern: "[What they actually mean]"
Best answer type: [Testimonial / Guarantee / Mechanism explanation / Case study / Risk reversal]
Where to handle: [VSL FAQ / Landing page guarantee section / Email X in sequence / Everywhere]

[Repeat for each Severity 3]

---

SEVERITY 2 OBJECTIONS (handle in copy or on call):

Objection: "[Exact wording]"
Best answer: [One-sentence direction]
Where: [Copy location or call stage]

[Repeat for each Severity 2]

---

SEVERITY 1 OBJECTIONS (minor - handled by standard proof and guarantee):
- "[Objection]" - Handled by: [what already in the funnel covers this]

---

PATTERNS:
Most common objection type: [Category]
Most dangerous objection: [The one that kills the most sales]
Objection the competitor handles best: [What they do well that we should match or beat]
Objection no competitor is handling: [The gap we can own]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] At least 8 objections documented, each written as the prospect would say it
- [ ] Every objection has a category, severity score, and source
- [ ] Real objection identified for all severity 2-3 items
- [ ] Copy/conversation direction written for all severity 2-3 items
- [ ] Pattern summary completed
- [ ] Output is specific enough that the Copywriter can write a FAQ section without additional research

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
