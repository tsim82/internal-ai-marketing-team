# Competitor Research

## Purpose

Produce a detailed competitor analysis that gives the team a clear picture of the competitive landscape: what competitors are saying, what is working for them, what their customers love and hate, and where the gaps are. This output feeds the Paid Ads Agent (angle differentiation), the Copywriter (positioning and language), and the Offer Agent (whitespace and unique mechanism).

## Used By

- Market Research Agent (primary)
- Copywriter (positioning and angle differentiation)
- Paid Ads Agent (ad angle research, what not to copy)
- Offer Agent (identifying whitespace)

## Trigger

Use at the start of any new campaign, when launching in a new niche, when performance drops and the market may have shifted, or when the client asks what competitors are doing.

## Inputs Needed

- List of 3-5 main competitors (names and URLs) - required
- Niche or product category - required
- Platforms they are advertising on (if known) - helpful
- Client's own offer positioning for comparison - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Identify the real competitors**
The client's list may not match who is actually competing for the same audience. Check:
- Who shows up in Google Search for the top 3-5 queries in the niche?
- Who is running ads in the Facebook Ad Library for relevant keywords?
- Who do buyers mention when discussing alternatives in reviews and forums?

Prioritize competitors by market visibility, not just who the client named.

**Step 2: Audit each competitor's primary offer and positioning**

For each competitor:
- Primary offer name and what it includes
- Main promise or claim (their headline or core pitch)
- Price or price range (if visible)
- Who they are targeting (language on their site, ad copy, testimonials)
- Unique mechanism or angle (what they say makes them different)
- Primary traffic source (organic, paid, or both)

**Step 3: Pull their ad copy**
Use the Facebook Ad Library for any competitor running paid ads. Document:
- Primary hook (first line of the ad)
- Angle type (problem, proof, curiosity, authority)
- Creative format (video, carousel, static)
- CTA
- Where the ad lands
- How long the ad has been running (30+ days usually means it is working)

**Step 4: Mine their reviews**
Reviews are the best available market research. Pull from Google Reviews, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Facebook Page reviews, YouTube comments on their videos.

For each competitor:
- Top 5-10 positive phrases (what customers rave about)
- Top 5-10 negative phrases (what customers complain about)
- Patterns that repeat across multiple reviews

Negative review patterns are particularly valuable - they show what the market is not getting from existing solutions, which is where differentiation lives.

**Step 5: Identify the whitespace**
After auditing all competitors:
- What promise is no one making that buyers clearly want?
- What objection is no one addressing that keeps appearing in reviews?
- What part of the experience do competitors consistently fail at?
- Is there an audience segment competitors are ignoring?

State the whitespace as one specific sentence: "No competitor in this market is addressing [X]. Buyers clearly want [X] because [evidence]. The opportunity is [specific angle or promise]."

**Step 6: Build the competitor matrix**
Create a comparison table scannable in under 2 minutes.

## Output Format

```
COMPETITOR RESEARCH - [Niche/Client] - [Date]

COMPETITORS ANALYZED:
1. [Name] - [URL]
2. [Name] - [URL]
3. [Name] - [URL]

---

COMPETITOR 1: [Name]

Primary offer: [Name and brief description]
Main promise: "[headline or core claim]"
Price: $[X]
Target audience: [description]
Unique mechanism: "[what they say makes them different]"
Primary traffic: [Paid / Organic / Both]

Ad copy (if running ads):
  Hook: "[first line of best-performing ad]"
  Angle: [Problem / Proof / Authority / Curiosity]
  Format: [Video / Image / Carousel]
  CTA: "[call to action]"
  Running: [how long - signal of performance]

Review analysis:
  Customers love: [Top 3 positive patterns with example quote each]
  Customers complain about: [Top 3 negative patterns with example quote each]

Assessment: [1-2 sentences on their market position]

---

[Repeat for each competitor]

---

WHITESPACE ANALYSIS:
Promise no one is making: [specific gap]
Objection no one is handling: [specific gap]
Audience no one is targeting well: [if applicable]
Experience failure point: [what competitors consistently fail at]

DIFFERENTIATION OPPORTUNITY:
"[One sentence: what this client can own in the market that competitors cannot or do not]"

---

COMPETITOR MATRIX (summary):
| Competitor | Main Promise | Price | Best Angle | Top Complaint |
|------------|-------------|-------|------------|--------------|
| [Name] | "[promise]" | $[X] | [angle] | [complaint] |
[Repeat]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] At least 3 competitors analyzed in detail
- [ ] Facebook Ad Library pulled for any competitor running paid traffic
- [ ] Review mining includes both positive and negative patterns with real quotes
- [ ] Whitespace analysis states the opportunity in one specific sentence
- [ ] Competitor matrix is complete and scannable
- [ ] Output is specific enough for the Copywriter to write differentiated copy without additional research
