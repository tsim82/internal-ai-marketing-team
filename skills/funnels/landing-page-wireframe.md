# Landing Page Wireframe

## Purpose

Produce a section-by-section wireframe for a landing page. The wireframe defines what appears in each section, in what order, and why. It is the document that tells the Copywriter what to write on each section and tells the designer or developer what to build. The wireframe is not the copy itself - it is the structure and content direction for each section.

## Used By

- Funnel Agent (produces the wireframe)
- Copywriter (writes copy for each wireframed section)
- Creative Design Agent (references for visual direction)

## Trigger

Use when building a new landing page or when an existing landing page needs restructuring because conversion rates are below target.

## Inputs Needed

- Funnel map (what this page's single goal is) - required
- Offer buildout (what is being promoted) - required
- Audience profile (awareness level, top objections) - required
- Traffic source (cold paid traffic, warm retargeting, email) - required
- Funnel goal (lead gen, application, direct sale, booking) - required
- Tech stack (what tool builds the page) - required

## Process

**Step 1: Confirm the single page goal**

Every landing page has one goal and one CTA. Before wireframing, confirm:
- What does the visitor do when they land here?
- What is the one action this page is optimized for?

If there is more than one CTA, the wireframe must explain how they align to the same conversion action.

**Step 2: Match the page structure to the awareness level**

The order and depth of sections changes based on how aware the visitor is:

- **Cold traffic (aware of problem, not your solution)**: Lead with the problem, build the case, introduce your solution, then ask for action. Longer page.
- **Warm traffic (aware of your solution)**: Lead with the outcome, brief mechanism, social proof, then CTA. Shorter page.
- **Hot traffic (have seen your offer, retargeting)**: Lead with the offer, include guarantee, strong CTA. Minimal page.

**Step 3: Define each section**

Standard sections for a lead gen landing page (cold traffic):

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Hero / Above the fold | Headline, subheadline, primary CTA. Must communicate the offer and who it is for in under 5 seconds. |
| 2 | Problem statement | Articulate the pain or situation they are in. Written in buyer language. No solution yet. |
| 3 | Solution intro / Mechanism | Introduce the offer and the mechanism that makes it work. Keep brief - not a sales pitch yet. |
| 4 | What you get | Bullet points or brief list of deliverables or outcomes. Specific, not generic. |
| 5 | Social proof | Testimonials, case studies, or data. Strongest proof first. |
| 6 | About / Credibility (optional) | Who is behind this and why they are qualified. Keep brief on cold traffic pages. |
| 7 | FAQ | Top 3-5 objections answered. |
| 8 | Final CTA | Repeat the offer and CTA. |

For application or book-a-call pages, add a section describing who is a good fit (and who is not).

**Step 4: Write the content direction for each section**

For each section:
- What is the goal of this section?
- What must the reader believe or feel after reading it?
- What key element is required (specific stat, testimonial, form, video)?
- Any notes for the Copywriter on angle or tone

**Step 5: Note mobile considerations**

Most landing page traffic is mobile. Flag:
- Hero CTA must be above the fold on mobile
- Long testimonials should be shortened for mobile display
- Form fields: minimize to name and email (or phone) unless more data is essential

## Output Format

```
LANDING PAGE WIREFRAME - [Client/Offer] - [Date]

PAGE GOAL: [One conversion action]
TRAFFIC SOURCE: [Cold paid / Warm retargeting / Email / Organic]
AWARENESS LEVEL: [Cold / Warm / Hot]
TECH TOOL: [ClickFunnels / GHL / Webflow / etc.]

---

SECTION-BY-SECTION WIREFRAME:

SECTION 1: HERO / ABOVE THE FOLD
Goal: Communicate offer and audience in under 5 seconds
Required elements:
  - Headline: [Direction for Copywriter - e.g., "Main benefit-driven headline for [audience]"]
  - Subheadline: [Direction - e.g., "Explains the mechanism in one sentence"]
  - Primary CTA button: [Label and where it goes - "Book Your Call" → Calendar]
  - Form: [If opt-in page: Name + Email only]
  - Visual: [Direction for Creative Design Agent]
Notes: [Any other direction]

SECTION 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT
Goal: [Buyer recognizes their situation and feels understood]
Required elements:
  - [3-5 bullet points or short paragraphs in buyer language]
  - No solution mentioned yet
Notes: [Source buyer language from audience research]

SECTION 3: SOLUTION / MECHANISM
Goal: [Introduce what makes this different from what they've tried]
Required elements:
  - [Mechanism name and brief explanation]
  - Why it works when other things haven't
Notes: [Keep brief for cold traffic - 100-200 words max]

SECTION 4: WHAT YOU GET
Goal: [Make the offer concrete and tangible]
Required elements:
  - [Specific deliverables or outcomes - not vague list]
  - [Include timeframe if applicable]

SECTION 5: SOCIAL PROOF
Goal: [Build belief through other people's results]
Required elements:
  - [2-3 testimonials or case study snippets]
  - Strongest result first
  - Testimonials that match different audience profiles
Notes: [Source from offer buildout proof section]

SECTION 6: FAQ
Goal: [Preemptively answer the top 3-5 objections]
Required elements:
  - [Top objections from objection research - list them here for Copywriter]

SECTION 7: FINAL CTA
Goal: [Give a clear, final reason to act now]
Required elements:
  - [Repeat the offer in one line]
  - Primary CTA button repeated
  - [Urgency or guarantee line if applicable]

---

MOBILE NOTES:
- CTA visible above fold on mobile: [Confirmed requirement]
- Form fields: [Minimum needed]
- Long testimonials: [Truncated version needed?]

---

COPY BRIEF FOR COPYWRITER:
Tone: [Conversational / Professional / Authoritative]
Awareness level: [Cold / Warm]
Top 3 objections to address in copy: [From objection research]
Buyer language notes: [Key phrases to use from audience research]
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Page has one goal and one primary CTA
- [ ] Section order matches the audience awareness level
- [ ] Each section has a documented goal and required elements
- [ ] Mobile considerations noted
- [ ] Copywriter brief included with tone and objections
- [ ] Social proof section specifies sources (not "add testimonials here")

## Status

Phase 3 complete.
