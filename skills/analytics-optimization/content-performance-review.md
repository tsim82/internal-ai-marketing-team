# Content Performance Review

## Purpose

Review organic content performance across social platforms and produce decisions on what to make more of, what to stop, and what angles to test next. Content performance review connects post performance to downstream outcomes (profile visits, DMs, link clicks, booked calls) where data allows. It is not a vanity metrics report.

## Used By

- Analytics Agent (primary)
- Social Media Agent (acts on the decisions)
- Copywriter (uses findings to brief higher-performing content types)

## Trigger

Use monthly as part of the regular reporting cycle, or when a content calendar is being planned and the team wants to make decisions based on what has worked.

## Inputs Needed

- Platform analytics data for the review period (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube) - required
- Post-level data, not just account-level - required
- Content calendar for the period (so each post can be matched to its pillar and format) - required
- Downstream data if available (link clicks, DMs, profile visits from specific posts) - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Pull post-level data for the period**

For each post, record:
- Date
- Platform
- Content pillar (Education, Social proof, Brand, Engagement, Offer)
- Format (static, Reel, carousel, text)
- Reach
- Engagement rate (interactions divided by reach, times 100)
- Saves (Instagram/LinkedIn)
- Comments and shares
- Plays and retention rate (if video)
- Link clicks (if applicable)

**Step 2: Sort by engagement rate**

Sort posts by engagement rate, not raw engagement. A post with 100 interactions from 200 reach outperforms one with 200 interactions from 5,000 reach.

Identify:
- Top 5 performing posts
- Bottom 5 performing posts

**Step 3: Find the patterns**

Group top performers by:
- Content pillar: Which pillars drove the most engagement?
- Format: Which formats outperformed?
- Hook type: What did the top-performing hooks have in common?
- Day/time: Did timing patterns emerge?

This pattern is what the Social Media Agent uses to brief future content.

**Step 4: Flag what to stop**

Content types or formats consistently in the bottom performers should be flagged for reduction or elimination.

**Step 5: Make format and pillar recommendations**

Based on the data, give specific recommendations for the next month:
- Which pillars to increase
- Which formats to prioritize
- Which hook angles to test
- What to deprioritize

## Quality Check

- [ ] Post-level data is used (not just account totals)
- [ ] Engagement rate is calculated (not raw numbers only)
- [ ] Top and bottom posts are identified
- [ ] Patterns are named specifically (not "education posts did well")
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable for the Social Media Agent and Copywriter
- [ ] Downstream data is included if available
