# VSL Script

## Purpose

Write the complete persuasive script for a Video Sales Letter (VSL). The script is the spoken text the presenter delivers. It follows the VSL structure and is formatted so the Video Agent can build a scene plan from it directly.

## Used By

- Copywriter (primary - writes the persuasive script)
- Video Agent (builds the scene plan from this script)
- QA Agent (reviews claims and voice before production)

## Trigger

Use when a new offer needs a VSL, an existing VSL is converting poorly, or the funnel type selection calls for a VSL as the primary sales mechanism.

## Inputs Needed

- Completed offer doc with unique mechanism, price, and guarantee (from Offer Agent) - required
- Audience profile and awareness level (from Market Research Agent) - required
- Objection map (from Market Research Agent) - required
- Target VSL runtime (10-20 minutes is standard for high-ticket) - required
- Brand voice rules - required
- Competitor research showing what else is in the market - helpful

## Process

**Step 1: Set the awareness level and opening strategy**
VSLs for cold traffic must earn attention before making claims. VSLs for warm traffic (email list, retargeting) can open with the offer context sooner. Determine this before writing line one.

**Step 2: Write the hook (first 30-60 seconds)**
The hook must stop the viewer from clicking away. Options:
- Result hook: Open with the most compelling client result. "One of our clients went from struggling to book sales calls to booked solid for six weeks in 30 days."
- Problem hook: Open with the specific frustration. "If you've spent money on ads and watched leads come in but never actually convert - this is for you."
- Curiosity hook: Open with a counterintuitive claim. "The reason most agencies fail their clients has nothing to do with traffic."

End the hook with a "stay for this" reason - a specific promise for what they get if they watch.

**Step 3: Write the problem section**
Describe the problem in detail using the buyer's own language from VoC research. The reader should feel the script was written for them. Cover:
- What the situation looks like right now
- What they have already tried that did not work
- Why those things did not work (sets up the unique mechanism)
- What it costs them to keep living with this problem

**Step 4: Write the credibility bridge**
Who is speaking and why should the viewer trust them? One paragraph maximum. Keep it about the viewer's problem, not a list of accolades.

**Step 5: Introduce the unique mechanism**
Name the specific approach, method, or system that makes this solution different from everything already tried. This is the concept behind the offer, not the offer itself.

"The reason [problem] keeps happening is [root cause]. What we discovered is that if you [mechanism], the result is [outcome] - without having to [thing they dread]."

**Step 6: Explain and demonstrate the mechanism**
Give enough detail to believe the mechanism works before asking them to buy:
- A brief case study or example
- Logical explanation of why it works
- Before/after contrast

**Step 7: Present the offer**
Name the offer and deliver the value stack. Each item: name, what it does, what it is worth. Build value before revealing price.

"When you join [Offer Name] today, here is everything you get: [Item 1 - what it does - $X value...] Total value: $[sum]."

**Step 8: Present the price with an anchor**
Pause before the price. Let the value land. "Today your investment is just $[price]." Anchor against the value stack and cost of inaction.

**Step 9: State the guarantee**
Specific, not vague. "If you do [specific action] and don't see [specific result] within [timeframe], we will [specific remedy]."

**Step 10: Handle the top 3-5 objections**
Write Q&A pairs for the most common objections from the objection map. Under 3 sentences each.

**Step 11: Write the final CTA**
Tell them exactly what to do. "Click the button below." Then add urgency if it is real and specific.

**Step 12: Write the close**
Confident, not desperate. "If this sounds like what you've been looking for, I hope to see you on the inside. If it's not the right fit, I understand. But if you're serious about [result], the time to move is now."

## Output Format

```
VSL SCRIPT - [Offer Name] - [Client]
Target runtime: [X-XX minutes]
Awareness level: [Level 1-5]
Traffic source: [Cold / Warm / Retargeting]

---

[HOOK - 0:00 to ~0:45]
[Spoken text]

---

[PROBLEM - ~0:45 to ~3:00]
[Spoken text]

---

[CREDIBILITY - ~3:00 to ~4:00]
[Spoken text]

---

[UNIQUE MECHANISM - ~4:00 to ~7:00]
[Spoken text]

---

[DEMONSTRATION / CASE STUDY - ~7:00 to ~10:00]
[Spoken text]

---

[OFFER REVEAL - ~10:00 to ~13:00]
[Spoken text including full value stack]

---

[PRICE PRESENTATION - ~13:00 to ~14:00]
[Spoken text - price reveal and anchor]

---

[GUARANTEE - ~14:00 to ~14:30]
[Spoken text]

---

[FAQ / OBJECTIONS - ~14:30 to ~16:30]
Q: [Objection]
A: [Spoken answer]
[Repeat for top 3-5]

---

[FINAL CTA AND CLOSE - ~16:30 to end]
[Spoken text]

---

SCRIPT NOTES:
Runtime estimate: [X minutes]
Claims requiring qualification: [list]
Placeholder items: [results, names, prices needing client input]
Hand to: Video Agent (scene plan) and QA Agent (claims review)
```

## Quality Check

- [ ] Hook earns attention in the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Problem section uses real buyer language from research
- [ ] Unique mechanism is clearly named and explained
- [ ] Value stack built before price is revealed
- [ ] Price anchored against value and cost of inaction
- [ ] Guarantee is specific, not vague
- [ ] All income/result claims are qualified or attributed
- [ ] No em dashes, no banned words
- [ ] Script reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] Estimated runtime is within target range
- [ ] Route to Video Agent and QA Agent before production
